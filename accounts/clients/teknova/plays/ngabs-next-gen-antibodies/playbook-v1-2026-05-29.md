# **1\. Why Teknova Fits the ngAbs Buyer**

Teknova is an ISO 13485-certified manufacturer of off-the-shelf and custom RUO- and GMP-grade reagents; buffers, media, cryopreservation systems (PluriFreeze), water, and custom formulations \-supporting programs from discovery through commercialization. For next-generation antibody (ngAbs) developers, Teknova's value is in the upstream and downstream process inputs that bispecific ADCs and multispecifics consume at scale: cell culture and expansion media, chromatography and TFF/UF-DF buffers, conjugation and formulation buffers, and cryopreservation media delivered RUO-through-GMP with fast custom turnaround.

The implication for targeting: the best-fit accounts are organizations actively developing ngAbs therapeutics that own or operate process development, bioprocessing, or manufacturing activity in North America \- not pure in-silico/discovery-only shops with no wet-lab consumption, and not accounts whose modality is unrelated to antibody-based biologics.

# **2\. ngAbs Modalities Workflow & Teknova Product Fit**

*This section maps each next-generation antibody modality to its production workflow and the Teknova products/services that fit each stage. Use it two ways: (1) as talking points for outreach, and (2) to confirm a lead's company actually runs steps that consume Teknova inputs (reinforces gate G4 in Section 5).*

All antibody-based modalities below share a common biologic backbone workflow: cell line/culture, expression, harvest, capture, polish, UF/DF, and formulation and then diverge in molecule-specific steps (e.g., conjugation for ADCs, chain-pairing control for bispecifics). Teknova's fit is the RUO and GMP-grade media, buffers, water, cryopreservation media, and custom formulations consumed across these stages.

## **2.2 Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs), incl. bispecific ADCs**

ADCs add a chemical conjugation step after the antibody is produced: the cytotoxic linker-payload is attached, then excess drug/solvent and off-target DAR species are removed, and the molecule is exchanged into a stable formulation. This conjugation \+ cleanup sequence is the highest-value, most buffer-intensive insertion point for Teknova.

| ADC-specific stage | What happens | Teknova product / service fit |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Reduction / interchain activation** | Reduce interchain disulfides (e.g., TCEP) or activate sites in a controlled buffer at defined pH; sometimes with transition-metal ions. | Conjugation reaction buffers — HEPES, histidine, PBS, MES at controlled pH (custom RUO/GMP); EDTA and salt solutions; reagent-grade water. |
| **Conjugation reaction** | React linker-payload with the antibody, typically in presence of organic cosolvent (DMSO/DMAc); maintain pH and homogeneity. | Custom reaction & quench buffers; pH-adjustment acids/bases; salt and additive stock solutions; high-purity water. (Teknova supplies the aqueous buffer system, not the cytotoxic payload.) |
| **Purification — TFF (UF/DF) and/or chromatography** | Remove free linker-drug, solvent, aggregates, and undesirable DAR species; exchange buffer. | Diafiltration buffers, IEX/mixed-mode wash & elution buffers, salt gradients (custom, GMP) in single-use bag formats; reagent-grade water. |
| **Formulation of the conjugate** | Stabilize a hydrophobic, aggregation-prone molecule; add surfactant/stabilizer; control pH. | Custom formulation buffers (histidine/citrate/phosphate) with surfactants (Tween-20, PEG) and stabilizers (sucrose, trehalose-type, glycerol); WFI-quality water; GMP grade. |

**Bispecific ADC note:** a bispecific ADC combines the chain-pairing complexity of a bispecific (Section 2.3) with the conjugation/cleanup chain above — meaning it draws on both buffer sets and is among the most reagent-intensive ngAbs workflows.

## **2.3 Bispecific & Multispecific Antibodies**

Bispecifics bind two targets; multispecifics bind three or more. Their defining manufacturing challenge is chain mispairing and assembly of non-native structures (e.g., knob-into-hole), which demands tightly controlled culture and extra polishing to separate correctly-paired product from mispaired species. There is no conjugation step (unless it is also an ADC), but upstream control and downstream polishing intensity increase.

| Bispecific/multispecific stage | What happens | Teknova product / service fit |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Optimized expression & chain-ratio control** | Tune media/feeds to balance chain expression and reduce mispairing; DOE-driven media work. | Custom-formulated mammalian cell-culture media, feeds, and supplement/amino-acid stock solutions tuned per DOE (custom service); WFI-quality water. |
| **Capture** | Protein A or affinity capture of the assembled molecule. | Equilibration/wash/elution/neutralization buffers (custom RUO/GMP); salt solutions. |
| **High-resolution polish (extra steps)** | Additional IEX/HIC/mixed-mode steps to separate correctly-paired from mispaired/aggregated species. | Multiple custom IEX/HIC buffer sets, fine salt gradients, pH-adjustment solutions; large-format bags for scale. |
| **UF/DF & formulation** | Concentrate and exchange into a stable final buffer. | Custom diafiltration & formulation buffers with surfactants/stabilizers; WFI-quality water; GMP grade. |

## **2.4 Other / adjacent ngAbs formats**

*These appear in lead lists under the ngAbs umbrella. They consume the same backbone reagents; qualify them when antibody-based and run with NA wet-lab/process activity.*

* **Antibody fragments & engineered scaffolds (scFv, Fab, VHH/nanobodies, BiTEs):** backbone culture \+ capture/polish \+ UF/DF \+ formulation buffers; often microbial expression → add microbial culture media, dry powders, and antibiotic solutions.

* **Fc-fusion & multispecific scaffolds:** same mammalian backbone reagents as bispecifics; custom media and polish buffers.

* **Immunocytokines / radioimmunoconjugates (conjugated antibodies):** ADC-like conjugation \+ cleanup buffer sets (Section 2.2), minus the cytotoxic payload supply.

* **Trispecifics / next-gen Fc engineered formats:** treat as multispecific (Section 2.3) with intensified polishing.

**Cross-modality summary:** Teknova's repeatable, high-volume fit across every ngAbs type is: custom-formulated cell-culture media & feeds (custom service, upstream), chromatography/TFF process buffers (downstream, catalog \+ custom), conjugation \+ formulation buffers (ADCs), PluriFreeze cryopreservation media (cell banking), WFI-quality/reagent-grade/PCR-certified water, and environmental-monitoring plates — all available RUO→GMP with custom formulation and Express•Tek fast turnaround.

# **6\. Contact-Level Screening**

## **6.1 LinkedIn verification (when a profile exists)**

A LinkedIn profile is NOT required for a lead to qualify. But when the database has one, screen it before approving the contact.

1. If a LinkedIn profile is present, open it and confirm the contact's MOST RECENT (current) job title and company match the data pulled from the database.

2. Match rule: current title maps to an approved title in the Section 4.2 table AND current employer equals the qualified company (Section 5).

3. On mismatch — e.g., the person has moved companies, changed roles out of scope, or shows no current role — flag the lead as STALE/MISMATCH and route it out of the active list (do not outreach on unverified data).

4. If NO LinkedIn profile exists, do not disqualify on that basis. Proceed using the database record, and tag the contact verification\_source \= database\_only for lower confidence weighting.

## **6.2 CRM activity suppression (hard rule)**

**Every lead must be screened against the CRM. Suppress (exclude from outreach) any contact who has had logged activity within the past 6 months.**

Count as disqualifying activity within the trailing 6 months (180 days from run date):

* Any outbound or inbound email logged to the contact

* Membership in any active or recent outreach campaign / sequence

* A documented meeting, call, or demo

* Any other logged touch (task, note tied to engagement, opportunity activity)

**Resolution & matching:**

* Match the contact in CRM by email first, then by full name \+ company as fallback.

* If a CRM match shows activity\_date within the last 180 days → SUPPRESS.

* If no CRM match, or the most recent activity is older than 180 days → ELIGIBLE on this check.

* When in doubt (ambiguous match), default to SUPPRESS and flag for human review — avoid double-touching a live contact.

# **7\. Screening Pipeline (developer logic)**

*Run the checks in order; short-circuit on the first failure to save lookups. Recommended sequence and outcome tagging:*

5. Company gates G1–G5 (Section 5). Fail → status \= disqualified company.

6. Title check against approved list (Section 4.2). Fail → status \= out of scope title.

7. LinkedIn verification if profile exists (Section 6.1). Mismatch → status \= stale mismatch; no profile → continue with verification\_source flag.

8. CRM 6-month suppression (Section 6.2). Activity found → status \= suppressed recent activity.

9. All checks pass → status \= qualified
