# **1\. Why Teknova Fits the mRNA Buyer**

Teknova is an ISO 13485-certified manufacturer of off-the-shelf and custom RUO- and GMP-grade reagents: buffers, biological buffers, salt and sugar solutions, WFI-quality water, dry powders, cryopreservation media (PluriFreeze), and custom formulations — supporting programs from discovery through commercialization with fast custom turnaround (RUO+ grade and Express•Tek production). For mRNA therapeutics developers, Teknova’s value sits across the full mRNA production workflow.

The mRNA process is reagent- and buffer-intensive at every stage, and most of these inputs are exactly what Teknova manufactures:

* **Upstream template:** Plasmid (DNA template) production: cloning, expansion, lysis, neutralization, and binding/elution buffers — Teknova publishes a dedicated Plasmid Workflow.

* **IVT:** In vitro transcription (IVT) and enzymatic steps: Tris, HEPES, and other biological buffers; salts (magnesium chloride, sodium chloride); nucleotide/additive solutions; nuclease-free / PCR-certified and WFI-quality water.

* **Downstream:** Purification & polishing: chromatography and Tangential Flow Filtration (TFF)/ Ultrafiltration- Diafiltration (UF-DF) buffers, salt and chaotropic solutions, surfactants/detergents for clarification and capture.

* **Formulation:** Lipid Nanoparticle (LNP) formulation & fill-finish: formulation, citrate/phosphate, and sucrose/sugar buffers; WFI-quality water in 1 L–200 L single-use bag formats for clinical-scale tech transfer.

* **Storage:** Cryopreservation & storage: PluriFreeze (RUO and GMP) and storage/stabilization buffers for drug substance and cell banks.

*Because Teknova supplies both RUO and GMP grades and offers custom formulation with fast turnaround, the strongest-fit accounts are organizations that consume process reagents repeatedly and at increasing scale as an mRNA program advances from discovery into process development, clinical, and commercial manufacturing in North America.*

**Exclusions — Disqualify at the company level**

* Discovery-only or purely computational/AI RNA-design shops with no wet lab or process-reagent consumption.

* Organizations whose only modality is unrelated to mRNA (e.g., small-molecule-only, antibody-only, AAV/gene therapy with no RNA program, diagnostics-only).

* siRNA / ASO / oligonucleotide-only companies with no messenger-RNA therapeutic program (different chemistry and workflow; out of scope unless an mRNA program is also present).

* Companies with no production or R\&D lab footprint in North America (see §4 geography gate).

* Distributors, pure research-tool resellers, and CROs with no relevant reagent consumption.

# **4\. Company Qualification Gates (must all pass)**

Every company must pass ALL gates below before any contact at that company is considered. Gates are deterministic gates, not scores.

| Gate | Rule | How to evaluate |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **G1 — Modality** | Organization is actively working on mRNA therapeutics. | Evidence of an mRNA/saRNA/circRNA program: pipeline page, publications, clinical trial listings, press/funding, or job posts referencing mRNA/IVT/LNP. Reject if the only modality is non-mRNA (antibody, small molecule, AAV, siRNA/ASO-only). |
| **G2 — Geography** | Production or R\&D lab in North America. | At least one physical lab/manufacturing site in US/Canada/Mexico. HQ abroad is fine if a NA site exists. Reject virtual-only / no NA wet-lab footprint. |
| **G3 — Lab/operations** | Runs physical R\&D or production labs. | Confirm wet-lab or manufacturing operations (PD, IVT, formulation, QC). Reject purely computational/AI, IP-licensing, or holding entities. |
| **G4 — Reagent fit** | Plausibly consumes Teknova-type reagents. | Implied by G1+G3 (mRNA process work ⇒ buffers, salts, water, formulation media). An existing-customer/billing flag in CRM is the strongest possible evidence and overrides website inference. |
| **G5 — Not excluded** | Not a disqualified type. | Apply §2 exclusions. NOTE: an existing-customer flag short-circuits the “reagent maker \= competitor” inference \- billing history beats scraped data. Such accounts are not excluded under G5 but still run G1 and the CRM activity rule. |

# **5\. Contact-Level Screening**

**5.1 Title / role match**

* Contact’s role maps to a target persona in §3.2 (normalize synonyms, abbreviations, and seniority). A Tier 4 or off-list title at a strongly-qualified account may still pass if it is the best available contact.

* Reject contacts whose role is clearly out of scope (e.g., facilities, legal, HR) unless they are the sole contact at a high-value qualified account, in which case flag for human review rather than auto-include.

**5.2 LinkedIn verification (when a profile exists)**

A LinkedIn profile is NOT required. When one is present, screen it before the lead is eligible:

* Confirm the contact’s MOST RECENT position matches the company in the database record (current employer, not a past role).

* Confirm the current job title matches (or is a reasonable synonym of) the title in the record.

* On mismatch (moved companies, changed roles, or stale title): mark linkedin\_verified \= false and route to human review; do not auto-include with conflicting employment data.

* On match: set linkedin\_verified \= true and capture the verification date and profile URL.

*If no LinkedIn profile exists, the contact remains eligible on other criteria; set linkedin\_verified \= null (not false) so “no profile” is distinguishable from “profile conflicts.”*

**5.3 CRM activity suppression (hard rule — trailing 6 months)**

Suppress any lead with logged CRM activity in the past 6 months. This protects live engagements from a cold/automated sprint.

**Every lead must be screened against the CRM. Suppress (exclude from outreach) any contact who has had logged activity within the past 6 months.**

Count as disqualifying activity within the trailing 6 months (180 days from run date):

* Any outbound or inbound email logged to the contact

* Membership in any active or recent outreach campaign / sequence

* A documented meeting, call, or demo

* Any other logged touch (task, note tied to engagement, opportunity activity)

**Resolution & matching:**

* Match the contact in CRM by email first, then by full name \+ company as fallback.

* If a CRM match shows activity\_date within the last 180 days → SUPPRESS.

* If no CRM match, or the most recent activity is older than 180 days → ELIGIBLE on this check.

* When in doubt (ambiguous match), default to SUPPRESS and flag for human review — avoid double-touching a live contact.

# **6\. Screening Pipeline (developer logic)**

Run in order. Short-circuit (stop and label) at the first failure so the disposition reason is unambiguous.

1. **Normalize:** Resolve & dedupe the company; attach CRM account if one exists (sets existing-customer flag).

2. **Company gates:** Apply company gates G1→G5. Fail any ⇒ disposition \= company\_rejected:{gate}.

3. **Title match:** Map contact title to a persona tier. Out-of-scope & not sole contact ⇒ contact\_rejected:title.

4. **LinkedIn:** If LinkedIn URL present, verify current employer \+ title. Conflict ⇒ route human\_review:linkedin\_conflict. Absent ⇒ linkedin\_verified \= null, continue.

5. **CRM 6-month:** Check contact AND account for activity in trailing 180 days. Hit ⇒ suppressed:crm\_activity.
