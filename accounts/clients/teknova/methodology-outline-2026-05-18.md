# AAV Gene Therapy Company List: Methodology and Evidence Trail

Prepared for the client conversation, 2026-05-18. This document shows exactly what we look for, where that definition came from, how it has changed, and what influenced every change. Every company on the list is traceable to a named public record.

---

## Part A: How a company gets on the list

### 1. Source from the authoritative public registry

Companies enter from clinicaltrials.gov, the U.S. government's clinical-trials registry. Every company is here because of a real, registered interventional trial, not a marketing page. This is the same source a regulator or investor would check.

### 2. Classify against your definition

The system applies the criteria your team defined (Part B below). It does not invent its own notion of "AAV gene therapy company."

### 3. Independently verify every flagged company

After the system flags a company, we open the actual trial record and confirm by inspection that it is a genuine AAV gene therapy program. We verify against the source, not against the system's own claim.

### 4. Evidence attached to every company

Each company shows the specific trial, what the treatment actually is, and why it qualified. Every entry is one sentence with a trial number behind it.

### 5. Honest classification

- **Confirmed** - verified against the trial record as a genuine AAV gene therapy company.
- **Not confirmed** - flagged by the system, but the evidence did not hold on inspection. Surfaced, not hidden.
- **Needs your review** - a domain-expert judgment call, routed to Ellie by design.

In this batch the verification step caught a real issue: for some companies the system had attached the wrong trial as the AAV evidence (a standard-drug or observational study) even though the company runs genuine AAV gene therapy programs in other trials. Verification corrected the evidence so every company points to an actual AAV treatment trial. Separately, several confirmed AAV companies are large global pharma (e.g. Pfizer) that fall outside the company-size and profile this offer targets; those are excluded by the targeting filter, not by questioning their science. Both are the methodology working as intended.

## Part B: The criteria, in detail

This is the working definition the system applies.

### What "AAV gene therapy company" means

**In scope:** a company that develops, manufactures, or contract-manufactures therapeutics that use Adeno-Associated Virus (AAV) as the gene therapy delivery vector.

**Not in scope:** other viral vectors (lentiviral, adenoviral), non-viral delivery, peptide / small molecule / RNA / autologous-cell modalities, and research-tools vendors that sell into AAV labs without running their own programs. Multi-vector contract manufacturers qualify only if AAV is one of their named services.

**A known trap we explicitly handle:** in clinical-trial data, "AAV" is also the abbreviation for ANCA-Associated Vasculitis, an autoimmune disease unrelated to the vector. This is the single largest source of false matches. A trial whose only "AAV" is the vasculitis disease is rejected, not surfaced.

### Company hard filters (all must hold)

- **Active AAV gene therapy work**, evidenced by the company's own pipeline, platform, or a registered trial.
- **Clinical stage:** at least one program preclinical through Phase II.
- **Company size:** under ~2,000 employees, or an AAV contract manufacturer of any size; not a wholly-owned subsidiary of a top-20 global pharma operating under that parent's manufacturing infrastructure.
- **Geography:** headquartered in the United States or Canada.

### The trial-evidence test (added 2026-05-18)

A company surfaced from trial evidence must sponsor a trial that satisfies all three:

1. It is an actual **interventional treatment** study (not an observational, antibody, seroprevalence, or registry study).
2. The condition is an AAV-relevant indication and is not the vasculitis disease.
3. The treatment itself is a **gene therapy product** (not standard-of-care drug, replacement therapy, placebo, or device).

A company is not confirmed just because it runs a trial in a disease where AAV is common. The cited trial must be an AAV treatment.

### Disqualifiers (removed regardless of other matches)

- Active business-development engagement on the account in the last six months.
- Already enrolled in another active outreach cadence.
- Stale or inactive contact employment.
- Acquired or operationally abandoned company (evaluated at the enrichment stage).
- Out-of-scope contact function or patient-facing clinical role.

## Part C: Where the criteria came from and how it changed

The criteria live in a single maintained document for this engagement. Every change is recorded with its source. Two forces shape it: your domain experts define what counts; verified public evidence sharpens precision.

| 4 | 2026-05-18 | Added the three-part trial-evidence test: the evidence cited for a company must be an interventional, AAV-indication, gene-therapy-product trial, not a weaker study that merely mentions the disease. | Hand-verification of all 35 surfaced companies against their trial records, which found that the system sometimes cited the wrong trial as a company's evidence and isolated that pattern. |

### What this means for trust

The definition is owned by your experts. Precision improvements are driven by checking real evidence, never by guessing. When the system surfaces something it should not have, we catch it at the verification step, record why, and tighten the criteria so it does not recur. The list improves under inspection rather than degrading.
