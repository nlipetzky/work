## The end-of-month milestone

A list of contacts you trust at 95%. A clean overlay of Salesforce activity so we are not reaching into accounts that are current customers or have active opportunities. Air-traffic control on saturation. These are the right outcomes.

Where I would push back, as a practitioner, is on the shape of the target. Contact data quality is not a single number across a list. The modern ABM stack treats it as a composite score per record across four dimensions, and sequences outreach by tier rather than gating the whole list at a threshold:

- **Data hygiene:** email deliverability, current employment, tenure in role
- **ICP fit:** function match, seniority match, segment fit
- **Suppression state:** no active SF opportunity, no current customer relationship, no DNC, no concurrent cadence, no recent BD contact
- **Signal and intent:** funding events, leadership hires, IND filings, conference activity, content engagement

A composite score sits over the top, and contacts move into tiers (A, B, C). Tier A goes first. The 95% framing is the right instinct in the wrong unit: what produces an outbound program you can stand behind is 95%+ data hygiene per Tier A record, 100% suppression, and a defensible composite score per contact.

What I would propose as the end-of-May milestone, and what I am willing and able to deliver, is a verified AAV Tier A cohort: roughly 20 to 40 companies confirmed AAV by Ellie, contacts sourced and scored across the four dimensions, Salesforce activity overlaid, saturation control wired into the send queue. Defensible. Achievable. A concrete result you can point to.

To deliver it, here is what each side carries:

- **On my side:** building the Salesforce activity overlay, sourcing contacts against confirmed AAV companies, scoring against the four dimensions, and wiring the send-queue saturation rules. The infrastructure already exists; this is reconfiguration on top of it.

## The process that produces these milestones

If this is how we want to engage, my recommendation is that we lock in the process below. The milestone is the unit of work, and everything else is in service of it.

- **The milestone.** One agreed deliverable per month, named in writing. It is the contract for the month.
- **The roadmap.** I build a roadmap of the sub-deliverables and integrations needed to produce the milestone, in order. You confirm it. It is the plan; once locked, it does not get renegotiated mid-month.
- **The priority surface.** A one-page doc in your Drive folder that acts as the weekly cursor on the roadmap. One item is active each week. The next week's item sits pre-populated as my default; you confirm it or pick something else from the roadmap.
- **The Wednesday update.** Progress against the roadmap, what is open on your side, what is open on mine, what is coming. The visibility layer, replacing the standing meeting.
- **Working sessions.** When there is concrete material to work through together. Not as a default cadence.

After the May milestone lands, we agree on the next one, I build the roadmap for it, and the cadence repeats. Every milestone is a node; the process is what produces them month after month.
