## How the work moves

Every play runs in two phases on top of the existing engine.

**Phase 1: Adapt the engine to the domain.** Every domain has its own specifics ... where the right data lives, how companies are labeled, what counts as a real signal. Phase 1 is finding those answers and tuning the engine accordingly. The work here scales with how unfamiliar the domain is: high-complexity domains take significant adaptation; adjacent domains require much less, because the engine already knows most of what is needed.

**Phase 2: Cycles.** Once the engine is adapted, the play runs in cycles ... sourcing, qualifying, refining, sending. Each cycle improves the rules. Cycles ship lists; adaptation does not.

One play is active at a time. A second starts once the first is past adaptation and into stable cycles.

## What the retainer produces each month

Your retainer gets you focused expertise on Teknova's outbound system. Each month, that produces:

- **One play moved forward.** Whether the play is in adaptation or in cycles, it advances meaningfully. In adaptation, the engine gets tuned to the domain ... sources identified, classification rules captured, matching logic built. In cycles, you see at least one list iteration plus the rule refinements that follow Ellie's review.
- **One integration, automation, or system build outside the active play.** Things that live in the broader Teknova system but are not part of the active play work ... a new Salesforce sync pattern, an automation to summarize a Teknova account, a new reporting view. Requested as needed, scoped together. Beyond one per month, additional work would be a scope conversation.
- **A weekly update from me** covering what was done, where things stand, and what is coming next.

This is what the cadence produces. When the cadence is held ... async written updates and focused work between them ... the retainer stays on production. When the cadence breaks down ... ad-hoc meetings, mid-cycle direction shifts, conversations about work still in motion ... what compresses is the production itself.

A note on meetings: they work when they have a defined purpose ... a decision to make, an artifact to review, a problem to work through together. They do not work as recurring status check-ins, because the weekly update already covers status. When something specific needs face time, we name it and convene. When it does not, we do not. The calendar does not drive the meetings; the work does.
