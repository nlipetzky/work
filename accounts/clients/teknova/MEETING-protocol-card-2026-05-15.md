# Monday meeting — reaction-protocol card (internal, Nick runs from this)

30 min, Google Meet. Audiences: **Ellie = output (her findings), Jenn = direction (perception of how you handle them)**. The card exists so routing is reflexive, not improvised.

## Before she speaks (the highest-leverage move)

Pre-frame the error budget out loud, first: *"This is a first pass from one source. We expect a few edge calls — the system is built to learn from exactly those. A small number is the model working, not breaking."* Said before review = a defect confirms the model. Not said = the same defect feels like failure. Same data, opposite meeting.

## The meeting is a DECISION meeting, not a trial

Three jobs only: (1) confirm the proof is recognized (fast — she pre-read), (2) route her findings to closure, (3) get the unlock: is company quality consistently good enough to scale downstream. The unlock is **recognition of proof, not permission to work**. Never say or imply "we're waiting on your sign-off." Production is always moving.

## Route every "this is wrong" into one of four lanes — do not solve live

1. **Judgment call that was always hers** (production-tool vs. gene-therapy): NOT an error. *"That's exactly the call we need from you — system correctly surfaced it as AAV; the gene-therapy/tool line is yours. Working as designed."* Her finding = the system working.
2. **Questioned rejection/omission** ("why isn't X here / why is Y out"): every verdict has a checkable reason. Show it. Sound → explain (20 sec, she agrees). Reveals a gap → *logged as a rule refinement* (the push-forward loop, by design). Either way: traceable, not a black box.
3. **Genuine misclassification (real defect)**: acknowledge clean, capture company + evidence as a specific logged item, state the correction path, **do NOT debug or defend live.** *"That's a real one — captured, here's how it sharpens the classifier, corrected next pass."* Closure without archaeology.
4. **Data hygiene** (duplicate, stale name): pre-owned. *"That's ours, handled downstream, not your review time."* Zero meeting minutes.

## Hard rules

- Never debug, defend, or fix in the room. Route to a lane, assign closure, move on.
- One defect ≠ the meeting. The pre-frame + the lanes are what stop one finding from dominating 35 right ones.
- Calm routing in front of Jenn reads as "mature system with a working loop." Improvised reaction reads as "not ready." The protocol is perception management as much as resolution.
- Close on the unlock + the staged-delivery line: company layer provable now → contacts/enrichment/Salesforce are routine → scale fast.
