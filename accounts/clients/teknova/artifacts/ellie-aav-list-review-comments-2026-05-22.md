## Patterns / takeaways

1. **Geography filter holds.** A majority of the drops are EU/UK/Asia manufacturing footprints (Myrtelle, Brain Neurotherapy Bio, AskBio France, MeiraGTx, Elpida, Baxalta, Biocad, NGGT, Novotech, Janssen K.K.).
2. **Asset-status drift.** Several drops are because the asset was terminated, divested, or failed (Janssen R&D, Baxalta, Biogen ocular, Excision EBT-101). The signal pipeline surfaced them as active; they aren't.
3. **Subsidiary noise.** Brain Neurotherapy Bio → AskBio → Bayer. AskBio France → US parent. Prevail → Eli Lilly. The domain resolver caught some of these, not all.
4. **CDMO vs sponsor distinction.** Ellie repeatedly flags that once a sponsor has selected a CDMO, the reagent buyer is the CDMO, not the sponsor (Myrtelle / Viralgen, Elpida / Catalent). This is a structural lens worth baking into the persona model.
5. **Net-new account suggestion.** Ellie proposed adding **Forge Biologics** (Columbus, OH CDMO) as a platform play. Not in the current list.
6. **Pipeline-vs-approved-product framing.** Both Regeneron and AbbVie keepers come with explicit instructions to lead with internal AAV science and avoid pitching the approved/partnered asset (Otarmeni, RGX-314).
