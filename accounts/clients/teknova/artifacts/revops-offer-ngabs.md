# Offer: ngabs

**Client:** teknova
**Play:** ngabs
**Date:** 2026-06-01

---

## Headline

Outreach to North American next-generation antibody developers — bispecific ADCs, multispecifics, and adjacent antibody-based formats — about Teknova's RUO-through-GMP custom reagent supply for the upstream, downstream, conjugation, and formulation steps these molecules consume at scale.

---

## Audience

The persona/role this offer is pitched to. Not a database segment.

- **Persona:** Process Development, Bioprocessing, Manufacturing Sciences (MSAT), and Downstream/Upstream leaders at ngAbs developers; plus R&D/science owners of ADC/conjugation and CMC; plus procurement leads sourcing R&D/GMP raw materials.
- **Context:** Works inside a biopharma, antibody-platform company, or CDMO that owns or operates wet-lab, PD, or GMP manufacturing activity in North America. Currently moving a bispecific, multispecific, ADC, or bispecific-ADC program through PD into scale-up, tech transfer, or clinical/commercial manufacture.
- **Pain we address:** The reagent supply chain for ngAbs is the hidden critical path. Mammalian cell-culture media must be custom-formulated to control chain-pairing for bispecifics; conjugation, polish, UF/DF, and formulation each demand specialty buffer systems at RUO→GMP grades; cryopreservation and water round out the consumable footprint. Off-the-shelf catalog reagents don't cover most of this, and slow custom-formulation vendors stall program timelines.

---

## The offer

What is being pitched, in substance.

- **Outcome:** A reagent supply partner that covers the full ngAbs workflow at the grade and turnaround the program needs — custom-formulated mammalian cell-culture media and feeds, process and chromatography/TFF buffers, ADC conjugation and formulation buffer systems, PluriFreeze cryopreservation media, WFI-quality and PCR-certified water, and environmental-monitoring plates — RUO through GMP, with fast custom turnaround so reagent supply stops being the gating step.
- **Mechanism:** ISO 13485-certified custom manufacturing of process inputs at the customer's specification, in formats from 25 mL to 200 L single-use bags, with animal-free options where required. Express•Tek fast-turnaround program for accelerated timelines. Catalog where catalog fits (process buffers, salt/sugar/additive stocks, water, cryopreservation media, agar plates); custom service where customization is the point (mammalian media, GMP buffer formulations).
- **Inclusions:** Cell-line development and banking reagents (PluriFreeze, custom media for banking); upstream expansion and production custom media, feeds, and supplements; harvest/clarification buffers; capture and polish chromatography buffer sets (Tris, HEPES, MES, citrate, phosphate, acetate); UF/DF and diafiltration buffers; ADC-specific reduction/conjugation/quench/cleanup buffers and aqueous reaction systems; formulation buffers with surfactants and stabilizers; WFI-quality and PCR-certified water; environmental-monitoring agar plates.
- **Pricing or commercial frame:** Not specified in the playbook. Standard custom-formulation engagement model assumed (scope-to-quote, RUO and GMP grade differentiated, single-use bag formats up to 200 L). Flag for sponsor confirmation before copy ships.

## Why now

Specific triggers and market context that make this play timely.

- **Trigger:** ngAbs are the most active modality in biopharma right now. Bispecific ADCs and multispecific antibodies have moved from discovery curiosities to Phase I-III pipelines across both clinical-stage biotech and large biopharma. Each program advancing through PD into clinical and commercial supply ramps reagent consumption sharply — and bispecifics + ADCs are the most reagent-intensive ngAbs workflows.
- **Window:** As long as the bispecific/multispecific/ADC pipeline expansion holds, which is structural rather than cyclical. Closer-in window: the engagement's wind-down end-date is 2026-06-25, so this play's send window inside the engagement is the four remaining sprints.
- **Context:** Mammalian media for ngAbs has to be custom-formulated to manage chain-pairing in bispecifics; this is not a catalog-SKU buy. Conjugation chemistry for ADCs requires controlled-pH aqueous buffer systems that catalog suppliers don't carry. Programs that hit reagent-supply bottlenecks at scale-up lose months. The playbook's framing is explicit: position cell-culture media as a custom project to scope, not an in-stock product to ship.

## Out of scope

Explicit boundaries.

- **Not pitched:** Cytotoxic linker-payload supply (Teknova provides the aqueous buffer system for conjugation, not the payload). Off-the-shelf mammalian cell-culture media as a stock SKU (the only catalog cell-culture item is PBS; mammalian media is a custom-formulation service). Distribution or resale of third-party reagents.
- **Not promised:** Specific turnaround times beyond what Express•Tek formally commits to. Off-the-shelf availability of custom-formulated media. GMP-grade output without the custom-formulation engagement.
- **Not for:** Discovery-only or purely computational/AI antibody shops with no wet-lab or PD consumption. Companies whose only modality is unrelated to antibody-based biologics (small-molecule-only, gene-therapy-only with no antibody program, diagnostics-only). Organizations with no production or R&D lab footprint in North America. Distributors and contract research orgs with no relevant reagent consumption.
