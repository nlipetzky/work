## Premise

The point of these drafts is to show, end-to-end, how the publication signals and the contact workflow combine into something Ellie can send. Each example uses a real person from the Primary list and a real signal from the Pfizer brief as the anchor.

What every example does:

1. Subject line names the program or operational moment.
2. Opening line proves we read what their team is actually doing right now.
3. Middle connects the signal to Teknova's offer (custom buffer and process reagent programs, lot control, supply continuity).
4. Ask is one specific, low-friction question.

What none of them do:

1. Lead with "we noticed your company does gene therapy."
2. Use generic value-prop language ("innovative solutions," "trusted partner," etc).
3. Pitch the product before pitching the conversation.

## What's deliberately omitted

**No "since you published X paper" line on the Chesterfield example.** That would be addressed to the analytical authors (Lerch, Powers, etc.) who are not Angela. Instead the copy uses the published method as upstream context for Angela's process chemistry work. That's the right connection.

**No mention of CIFFREO or the DMD program discontinuation.** That's a sensitive moment for the people who worked on it and not a comfortable lead with someone we don't know.

**No price, no product names, no "our solution."** The conversation goal is one 20-minute call, not a quote.

## How this scales

Each Primary contact gets one copy variant matched to:

- Their function (MSAT vs process chemistry vs drug product)
- The most relevant active program for that function
- The operational moment (commercial supply vs filing prep vs long-term cohort)

The publication brief feeds the signal anchor. The contact workflow feeds the recipient. Same workflow, different program contexts, same 4-paragraph structure.

For Ellie: these are starting points, not final sends. Adjust tone to match Teknova's voice and any prior account history.
