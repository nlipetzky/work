# Contact Discovery: What the Curated List Produced

## Headline

The May 22 curated list converted at 75%. 18 of 24 companies returned at least one targeted contact under the AAV-CMC persona. Six did not. Of those six, five sit at the small-biotech end of the AAV-CMC market, and the sixth, Alexion/AstraZeneca, is a separate case at the large-pharma end.

The list reflects Ellie's curation. Every company that went through contact discovery was a keeper after her May 22 review, and every company she scrapped was held out.

## What returned, in volume order

Eli Lilly (73), AbbVie (63), Sarepta (20), Rocket (14), Sangamo (14), REGENXBIO (7), BioMarin (7), Adverum (4), Atsena (4), PTC (4), Apertura (2), Opus (2), Taysha (2), Abeona (2), Affinia (1), Astellas (1), Neurogene (1), Passage Bio (1).

## Why the six returned zero

**Five small biotechs: IVIEW, Aspa, Sardocor, Benitec (36 employees), Tern.**

The persona is built around the titled CMC owner: a Director or VP of process development, drug product, manufacturing science, viral vector, or CMC, located in the United States or Canada. At a 9-to-36-person biotech, that role usually has not been hired into a discrete title yet. The person making the reagent decision still exists — it is the CSO, the CEO, the scientific founder, or the operations lead — but their title does not match the AAV-CMC persona, and a persona-driven search will not surface them. When a match does appear, the function-exclude rules (Regulatory, QC, QA, Clinical Operations) often remove it. Benitec produced exactly one Apollo result, titled "SVP CMC Regulatory" — kept by the CMC inclusion, removed by the Regulatory exclusion.

This is the AAV-CMC market reading itself back to the persona. The persona is correctly aimed at the function. At small-biotech scale, the function has not yet been spun out from the founding team into a titled hire.

**One large-pharma case: Alexion/AstraZeneca (76,100 employees).**

Companies above 500 employees were intentionally subject to a tighter filter (US/Canada location, tenure of five years or more, publication history). At Alexion's size, that filter narrows the searchable population substantially, and the current discovery run does not return contacts that pass it. This is a known limitation of the dataset as handed off.

## What this says about the addressable AAV-CMC universe

The 75% conversion across Ellie's curated list is the operative number. It means the persona-plus-curation system surfaces a working contact at three of every four AAV-CMC targets the expert validates. The misses are not random — they cluster at the structural extremes of the market (very small or very large), and both extremes are known.

The small-biotech end surfaces a real trade-off worth naming. This engagement was scoped to build a broad, enriched, persona-driven outreach list across the AAV-CMC segment. That approach serves the 18 companies that have a titled CMC owner. The five small-biotech zeros sit in a part of the segment where the buyer is real but untitled — typically the CSO, CEO, or scientific founder. Reaching that buyer requires per-account intelligence and custom messaging, not persona matching. Same overall ABM scope, different operating mode underneath.

Stated cleanly: a list-plus-persona play covers most of the curated universe. The pre-titled tail needs a named-individual play on each account. Both are valid; they are different units of work.

## Decisions available

These are options if the discovery is rerun. None require action.

1. Hold the persona as written. Accept the 75% conversion and treat the small-biotech zeros as a known characteristic of the segment.
2. Loosen seniority for companies under 50 employees, allowing Manager and Head titles in addition to Senior, VP, and Director. This catches the cases where a titled hire has happened below Director level.
3. Treat the function-exclude rules as a primary-function check rather than a string match, so compound titles like "SVP CMC Regulatory" remain in.
4. Treat the small-biotech tail as a separate named-individual play, sourcing the CSO, CEO, or scientific founder per account rather than searching by persona. This is a different operating mode and a different unit of work, not a tuning of the current rules.

Options 2 and 3 are persona refinements; option 4 is a scope choice about whether the small-biotech tail belongs in the list-build play at all. Doing 2 and 3 together would likely move 2 to 3 of the five small-biotech zeros into a returnable state. Doing 1 leaves the dataset as the handoff record.

## Closing

We have capacity in the current sprint to apply any of these decisions and rerun the affected subset, if useful. Otherwise the dataset as delivered stands as the handoff record, with the limitations above documented.
