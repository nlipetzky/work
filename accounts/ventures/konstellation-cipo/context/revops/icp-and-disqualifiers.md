# ICP & Disqualifiers — curated source material

Provenance: Will's "IP Intelligence Platform" doc (2026-05-19, adapted to
Konstellation) + 2026-06-09 / 06-23 transcripts + SME credibility map
(../../konstellation-ai/artifacts/sme-credibility-will-rosellini-v0-2026-05-27.md).

## Who it's for (ICPs by stage, mapped to tiers)
- **Pre-seed/seed startup** (0-2 provisionals, preparing for seed/Series A, needs
  investor-ready IP positioning) → Scout.
- **Post-seed / Series A** (active prosecution, approaching commercial launch, needs
  FTO clearance + competitive positioning) → Shield.
- **Growth-stage / Series A-B** (5+ patent families, licensing/M&A/international
  expansion underway) → Arsenal.
- From the transcripts: the entry signal is the inventor/founder who believes "my
  patent is infringed," but the durable need is portfolio management and ongoing IP
  strategy. Will notes ~4 ICPs within this one unmet need; he's had ~100
  conversations and the model is already producing ~$20k/mo.

## Fit signals
Venture-backed or fundraising; technology-differentiated; IP is material to
valuation/defensibility; capital-constrained enough that hourly IP billing is a
barrier.

## Disqualifiers (hard)
- **Law firms / IP-services firms as buyers.** Will's hard exclusion (SME
  credibility map). Do not target or pitch law firms; reframings ("hypothetically",
  "as an example") do not unlock it.
- Buyers wanting pure patent *prosecution/filing* — Will: "no margin there," not the
  offering (Konstellation is intelligence + CIPO advisory, not a filing shop).
