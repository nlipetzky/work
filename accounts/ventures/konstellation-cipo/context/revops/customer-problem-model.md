# Customer Problem Model — curated source material

Provenance: Will's "IP Intelligence Platform" doc (2026-05-19, adapted to
Konstellation) + 2026-06-09 transcript.

## The problem (causal model, why it persists)
Traditional IP consulting is structurally broken for early-stage companies:

1. **Hourly billing kills trust.** IP attorneys quote $500-800/hr with no ceiling.
   The rational founder response under capital constraint is to defer. Result:
   last-minute provisionals, skipped FTO, blocking patents discovered only when a
   competitor sends a cease-and-desist — by which point remediation cost dwarfs what
   proactive strategy would have cost.
2. **Episodic engagements create blind spots.** Project-based consulting means a
   startup gets one report, then silence. Meanwhile competitors file, the landscape
   shifts, and the IP position erodes unnoticed. No continuous intelligence layer.
3. **IP is mis-framed as a cost center.** Because it's priced like litigation
   (opaque hourly, unpredictable scope), it gets bucketed with legal expense rather
   than as R&D / valuation-driving investment. The pricing perpetuates the framing.

## Why it has not been solved
The firms that could give strategic IP guidance price themselves out of the startup
market; self-service patent tools have no strategic/advisory layer. Nobody combined
agentic analysis + human CIPO judgment at a startup-accessible price. (Per the
06-09 transcript: inventors fixate on "my patent's infringed," but the real
recurring need is portfolio management and ongoing IP strategy.)

## What it costs the customer
Deferred IP work → weak portfolio → lower valuation, weaker fundraise, exposure to
blocking patents and litigation. The cost of the blind spot is paid later, larger.
