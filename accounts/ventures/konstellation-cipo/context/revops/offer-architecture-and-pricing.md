# Offer Architecture & Pricing — curated source material

Provenance: Will's "IP Intelligence Platform" pricing doc (2026-05-19, adapted from
PatentVest to Konstellation) + 2026-06-09 / 06-23 transcripts. Pricing is Will's own;
public-facing pricing routes through Will.

## The packaging (subscription tiers)
- **Scout — $2,500/mo** (pre-seed/seed): 2 agentic deliverables/mo, competitor
  monitoring (up to 5), monthly intel digest, 1×30-min CIPO advisory call.
- **Shield — $5,000/mo** (Series A): 5 deliverables/mo (full menu incl. FTO),
  monitoring (up to 10), bi-weekly updates, quarterly portfolio review, 2 advisory
  calls, investor IP-deck prep.
- **Arsenal — $10,000/mo** (growth): unlimited deliverables (20-workflow suite),
  unlimited monitoring, weekly briefings + CIPO calls, board memos, M&A IP due
  diligence, licensing/monetization strategy.
- Annual prepay = 10% discount.

## À la carte credits
$500/credit, mapped to deliverables (e.g. Prior Art Search 2cr, FTO 5cr, IP
Valuation 8cr, Capital Strategy 10cr). Packs of 10/25/50 at 10/20/30% off. Credits
never expire, shared across an org. Gateway: credit buyers convert to subscription
(~40-60% in 6 months).

## Outcome-aligned layer (optional, on top of subscription)
Success fees: licensing 5-10% of deal value; M&A 1-3% of IP-attributed value;
investor uplift 2%; gov't grant IP 5%. And a Revenue Participation Agreement
(perpetual 0.5-1.5% royalty on revenue from IP Konstellation helped develop) offered
to Arsenal, traded for reduced/waived upfront fees.

## Pricing logic (why the price is the price)
The agentic layer collapses cost-to-deliver (FTO ~$200-500 vs traditional
$4-8k), so 80-90% gross margin; one CIPO serves 30-50 clients (vs 8-12 traditional).
Tiers map to startup stage and ability to pay; credits remove commitment friction;
outcome fees signal confidence and align incentives. Anchored against hourly IP
billing ($350-800/hr) which startups defer.
