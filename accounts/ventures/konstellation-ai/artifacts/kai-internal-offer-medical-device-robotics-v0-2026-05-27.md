## Correction note (v0 → v0.1)

v0 of this artifact framed the offer as "Konstellation sells a Diagnostic; Will is the credibility marker on the proof line." That was wrong. The cold-outreach reality for this play is the inverse: **Will is the offer.** The buyer replies because they want time with Will Rosellini specifically. Konstellation is the operating layer that makes Will's time leveraged ... it's how he can talk to 30 medical device robotics CEOs in a quarter instead of three. The buyer doesn't need to learn about Konstellation in the cold message. The buyer needs to want to meet Will.

This v0.1 rewrites the offer around Will-as-the-asset. The Diagnostic still exists, but it's the **conversion step**, not the cold pitch.

Will Rosellini ... first US FDA approval of an AI medical device, IP attorney turned operator, has bought and grown medical-device companies, currently spending the next 90 days talking with medical device robotics CEOs about their commercial trajectory ... wants 30 minutes with you.

Worth 30 minutes?

## What the buyer is actually being offered

This is the part v0 missed. The asymmetry is the point.

**The buyer gives:** 30 minutes of their time. That's the entire cost.

**The buyer gets:** Time with a peer who has actually been on the other side of their problem. Specifically:

1. **Pattern recognition from someone who's audited many of these companies.** Will has bought and grown medical-device businesses. He's seen the failure modes from the inside. In 30 minutes, the buyer gets a peer-level read on what's structurally broken in their commercial motion ... not a vendor's pitch dressed up as advice.

2. **Validation or honest disagreement from a credentialed peer.** First US FDA approval of an AI medical device. The buyer is talking to someone who's done what they're trying to do. The buyer's engineering, clinical, and regulatory bets get pressure-tested by someone who passed the same gates.

3. **Optionality on Will's network.** Will has spent a decade in commercialization, IP, and capital allocation. The buyer is one warm intro away from people they'd otherwise spend a year trying to reach. Will doesn't open his network for everyone, but he opens it for people he thinks are interesting.

4. **A possible M&A or capital signal.** Will is actively in market looking at acquisitions in medical device robotics. The buyer doesn't have to be selling. But the conversation might surface alignment that doesn't surface in a vendor call. Worst case: peer conversation. Best case: a multi-million-dollar relationship.

5. **A real answer to "what would I do differently if I were running your commercial layer."** Most CEOs in this segment have never had that conversation with a peer who's actually done it. The conversation itself is the value.

**The buyer doesn't get (and doesn't expect, from a cold message):** A pitch for services. A sales close. A demo. A pricing conversation.

If the conversation surfaces a real pipeline gap that fits Konstellation's operating model, Will brings up the Diagnostic. If it doesn't, the conversation is the deliverable. Either way, the buyer's 30 minutes is worth it.

## Why a med device robotics CEO replies yes

Put yourself in the seat of a CEO/Founder of an $18M revenue surgical robotics company. They get 200 cold messages a week. 199 of them are some flavor of "we help companies like yours generate leads with AI." They delete those without opening.

The message that lands is the one from someone they would actively want to meet for non-vendor reasons:

- A peer who passed the FDA approval they're trying to pass
- An IP attorney who has fought patent fights they'll probably fight
- An operator who has bought businesses their size
- Someone in market right now looking at companies like theirs
- Someone whose 30 minutes might lead to ... anything. Capital. M&A. Honest pattern recognition. A warm intro. A board referral.

The buyer doesn't have to know what they want from the conversation. They just have to know talking to Will is a fundamentally different category from talking to a vendor.

That's why the offer works. Cold outreach selling "AI-powered lead generation services" gets a 0.5% reply rate from this audience because every CEO has been burned by an agency. Cold outreach offering "30 minutes with the guy who got the first AI medical device approved" is a different ask entirely.

## What's actually being sold (the structural layer ... not for cold copy)

The Diagnostic and the Operated RevOps Cluster are still the commercial structure. They just don't live in the cold pitch. The flow:

```
Cold message: "30 min with Will?"
    ↓
Buyer replies yes (high rate, because peer conversation, not vendor pitch)
    ↓
Will runs the 30 min as a peer conversation, not a sales call
    ↓
Will diagnoses the commercial layer in real time
    ↓
IF buyer's gap fits Konstellation's model:
    Will offers the GTM Diagnostic (paid, two weeks, productized)
    "If you want help fixing this, we run a paid Diagnostic ...
     two weeks, ends with a system design and a Trajectory.
     If you don't agree with what we recommend, we part ways."
    ↓
IF buyer accepts Diagnostic:
    Diagnostic delivered
    Continuation as Operated RevOps Cluster (BWY default)
IF not:
    Conversation ends as a peer conversation; buyer might come back later;
    Will captures Learnings for the Network Map and Pattern Library
```

The Diagnostic is the **conversion gate**, not the **cold offer**. The cold offer is Will's time.

## Audience (who this is for, refined for the reframed offer)

Founder, CEO, or commercial executive at a North American medical device robotics company, $10M to $100M in revenue. Most-promising sub-segment:

- **Founder-CEO at $10-30M** ... the peer-fit is sharpest here. They are still effectively running commercial because there is no commercial exec between them and revenue. They are exactly the person Will was several years ago. They will reply because they want to talk to their slightly-further-down-the-path counterpart.
- **CCO / VP Sales at $30-100M** ... different conversation. They want to talk to Will because he's been on the commercial side of an FDA-AI approval and they need to understand what that does to the commercial conversation. They will reply because Will has insight they cannot get from another vendor.

Both replies are valuable. The copy can vary by tier.

## Why now

- **Will is genuinely in market right now.** Not a rhetorical "now." He has a 90-day window where he's specifically focused on medical device robotics conversations. The buyer who replies in the next 60 days gets time; the buyer who replies six months from now gets a different person with a different focus.
- **The agentic-AI commercial shift is happening.** Buyers using their old marketing playbook are starting to lose to buyers using a new one. Will is one of the few people in this space who can speak to both sides (the AI-in-the-medical-device side AND the AI-in-the-commercial-layer side).
- **The buyer's revenue band is the sweet spot for honest conversation.** Below $10M they don't have leverage; above $100M they're locked into an enterprise sales motion that nobody can fix in 30 minutes.

## Why this isn't generic peer-to-peer cold outreach

Plenty of operators do "I'm a peer, want to chat" cold outreach. Three things keep this from being generic:

1. **The credential is specific and verifiable.** Not "I've helped companies like yours." Will got the first US FDA approval of an AI medical device. That's a concrete, falsifiable, peer-defining claim.
2. **The window is real.** Will is actually in market for 90 days, actually allocating capital, actually running this specific cohort of conversations. The urgency isn't manufactured.
3. **The asymmetry is honestly stated.** The buyer gives 30 minutes; the buyer gets pattern recognition + optionality. There's no hidden ask. The Diagnostic only comes up if the buyer's situation fits it.
