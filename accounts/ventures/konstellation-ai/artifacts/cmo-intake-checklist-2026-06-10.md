# CMO Intake Checklist — Will Walkthrough
**Date:** 2026-06-10, 10:00 a.m. central, 90 min
**Format:** Will drives the walkthrough. Nick interrupts with sharp questions when something is vague. Output is enough clarity for Nick to start building the Make-a-Promise layer in increments.

## Frame (Nick's pocket, don't say out loud)

- Will already has Record a Promise dialed. You own Make a Promise.
- He explicitly told you: don't overwhelm, don't build it all at once, give him one thing at a time.
- Your job today is extract, force precision, capture. Build later.
- Anchor question to keep asking: "What do you actually mean by that?"
- If you catch yourself proposing solutions, stop. Today is diagnosis.

## Success criteria (what you walk out with)

- Four sub-ICP profiles, each specific enough to start segment criteria work
- Message inventory: what's worked in the 100 conversations, what hasn't
- One or two channel bets to lean into
- A pipeline shape (stages + advancement criteria)
- A capacity ceiling: how aggressive can lead volume go before delivery breaks

## 1. North Star + revenue math (5 min)

- What's the revenue target you're solving for by Q3? By year-end?
- $3k/month average × how many subscribers gets you there?
- What's the worst-case conversion rate from cold conversation to paid that you'd still ship against?
- If you only solved one of the four ICPs, which one would you pick first and why?

**Watch for:** vague targets ("a lot"), no math behind the number. Force a number.

---

## 2. The four ICPs (25 min, biggest block)

For each of the four, force these answers. If Will can only answer for two, that's a finding.

- Who exactly? Title, role, company size, sector. Name three real people who fit.
- What do they call the problem in their own words? (Not your words.)
- What's the trigger event that makes the problem urgent right now? (New filing? Litigation threat? Patent expiring? Competitor product launch?)
- How do they currently try to solve it? What do they pay for today?
- Where do they already congregate? Associations, publications, conferences, online communities.
- What proof do they need to take a meeting with you? To sign $3k/month?
- Are they buying alone, or do they have to convince a lawyer / CFO / board?

**Watch for:** "inventors" without a tighter cut. "Inventors" is a population, not an ICP.
**Watch for:** the four blurring into one. If you can't tell ICP 1 from ICP 3, neither can the market.

---

## 3. Message + proof (15 min)

- Say your one-sentence pitch out loud right now. (Then: where is it jargon, where is it clear?)
- What language do prospects use that you're not echoing back yet?
- What language are you using that prospects never say?
- From the 100 conversations: which proof points moved someone? Which fell flat?
- What's the single most common objection? What's your current answer to it?
- What's the one case study or story you tell first? Why that one?

**Watch for:** "AI-powered" type framing. Prospects don't buy AI, they buy outcomes.

---

## 4. Channel (15 min)

- Where did the 100 conversations actually come from? Break it down by source.
- Which source converted highest? Which converted zero?
- LinkedIn just went 2k → 20k impressions. What's the content cadence right now? Is there one?
- Is email in play? Sequences set up? Domains warm? Sender reputation clean?
- Podcasts, panels, publications, events on the calendar? Anything that's a forcing function for inbound?
- What's a realistic weekly volume of NEW first conversations you'd want to handle?

**Watch for:** "LinkedIn is working" without a tracked source breakdown. Anecdote isn't data.

---

## 5. Pipeline state (10 min)

- Of the 100 conversations, how many are still warm right now?
- Where do they currently live? CRM, notes, memory, inbox threads?
- What are the stages from cold to paid? Define each one.
- What's the criterion for advancing a prospect from stage N to stage N+1?
- What's the typical time-to-close on the closes you have?
- Where does the pipeline leak hardest?

**Watch for:** "I have it all in my head." If it's in his head, it doesn't scale and you can't help.

---

## 6. Offer precision (5 min, locked, just verify)

- $3k/month, 5 or 10 agents. What determines which tier a prospect lands on?
- What is the CIPO doing per month, concretely? Synthesis call cadence?
- What's IN the subscription? What's explicitly OUT?
- Annual term? Month-to-month? Cancellation policy?
- The half-cash / half-public-stock idea you raised: when do you offer that, and to whom?

**Don't redesign anything here.** Just make sure you understand what you're going to be marketing.

---

## 7. Brand + identity (5 min)

- Confirm: this is sold under "Will Rosellini" personal brand, not Konstellation, for now?
- Where does Konstellation get credited? Footer? Contract? Nowhere visible yet?
- What's the URL someone lands on after they hear you talk? Is that URL going to embarrass you in front of a paying prospect?
- LinkedIn content engine: who's writing? What's the cadence? Is there a system, or is it ad hoc?

---

## 8. Delivery capacity (10 min)

- How many active subscribers can you support today, alone?
- The ~15 agents you've built: what does each one do? Which ones touch the client directly?
- At 20 subscribers, what breaks first? Sales follow-up? Synthesis call quality? Ops?
- At 50, what breaks? At 100?
- What's the hire-or-build threshold for offloading work from Nick or you?

**This is the constraint on how hard the lead-gen layer can push.** Don't skip it.

---

## 9. Constraints + boundaries (5 min)

- What are you not willing to do? Cold email blast? Pay-per-lead? Affiliate?
- Anything off-brand or off-tone you'd refuse?
- Anything legally sensitive about the patent / litigation space we have to walk around?
- Anyone you'd refuse to work with, individually or by category?
