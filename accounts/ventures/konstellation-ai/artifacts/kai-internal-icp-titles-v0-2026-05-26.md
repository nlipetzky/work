## Decision (what binds to the sourcing engine)

For the KAI Internal Medical Device Robotics play, the sourcing engine pulls contacts in these tiers from companies matching the play's segment criteria (medical-device-robotics-where-the-robot-IS-the-device, $10M-$100M revenue, North America).

### Tier A ... primary outreach

- Founder, Co-Founder
- CEO, President
- Chief Commercial Officer
- VP Sales, VP of Sales, SVP Sales

### Tier B ... secondary; surface only when Tier A is blank at the account

- Chief Growth Officer
- Head of Business Development, VP Business Development
- General Manager (business-unit lead, if the company is multi-product)

### Skip ... do not surface to the outreach list

- CTO, VP Engineering, VP R&D, VP Product ... wrong pain
- Chief Medical Officer, VP Regulatory, VP Clinical ... clinical and regulatory seats
- VP Marketing ... flag for manual review only when no Tier A or Tier B exists at the account; typical view in this category at this size is a weak or absent commercial marketing seat

## Rationale

The pain Will is selling against ("where are my leads") sits in two different seats depending on company size inside the $10M-$100M band:

- **Lower band ($10-30M).** Founder is still the de facto head of GTM. Often a technical or clinical founder. The pain hits them directly because there is no commercial executive between them and revenue. Will's credential (first US AI medical device approval, IP and commercialization background) punches as a peer.
- **Upper band ($30-100M).** A commercial layer has formed. CCO or VP Sales owns the pipeline pain and the budget. Will pitches at peer level commercially.

## Sequencing

Launch order for outreach:

1. **Founders and CEOs at $10-30M companies first.** Highest peer-credential match. Fastest decision cycles. Tightest learning loop for the first 30 calls Will targets (see Play definition and Trajectory).
2. **CCOs and VP Sales at $30-100M companies second.** Add only after the message has been validated against the founder-CEO cohort. The pitch shape (lead with outcome, gate on Diagnostic) likely needs different proof points to land with a commercial executive vs. a founder; treat the second cohort as a separate test.
