## Touch 1 ... Connection request note

> "Watching medical device robotics commercial motions split since FDA started clearing AI software. Pre-clearance companies aren't selling the same way as post-. Open to a quick exchange on what you're seeing?"

**Josh Braun (Sales Without Sleaze):** Tonally good. It's an observation, not a pitch. But "Open to a quick exchange on what you're seeing?" is still an ask. Test going softer: state the observation, end on a soft permission gauge like "Curious if you're seeing it too?" or drop the CTA entirely. *The connect note's job is to get accepted ... LinkedIn's Accept button IS the CTA.*

**Lavender (Will Allred):** Hook lands in the first 50 characters (good). Timeline hook (good ... per the data, timeline hooks beat problem hooks ~2x for CEO/founder audiences). One critique: "medical device robotics" is the third word. Move it to the first word so the prospect knows in 5 characters this is about THEIR world. Try: "Medical device robotics companies are splitting into pre- and post-FDA-AI-clearance commercial motions. They're not selling the same way. Open to comparing notes?"

**Becc Holland (Flip the Script):** Where's the YOU trigger? This goes to any robotics CEO. For v0 mass-send I accept the trade. Flag for v0.2: per-recipient personalization at the opener (one line referencing this company's recent funding, product approval, hire, or LinkedIn post) would 2-3x the accept rate. Build the AI-personalization layer next.

**Belal Batrawy (Death to Fluff):** "Quick exchange" is fluff. "Open to" is overused. Try "Worth comparing notes?" Tighter.

**Jason Bay (Outbound Squad):** Strong opener. Problem-first frame, prospect-focused, no pitch. *I'd test removing the CTA entirely ... let the connect button carry it.*

## Touch 2 ... First DM after accept

> "Thanks for connecting. Quick context. I'm one of the small group of operators who's been through FDA clearance for an AI medical device, and I'm using a 90-day window to compare commercial-trajectory notes with CEOs running med device robotics companies in the $10-100M band. The pattern I'm finding: post-AI-clearance sales cycles aren't behaving like pre-AI ones. Payer conversations shift, the evidence ask changes, the cycle stalls in a different place. I'd trade you what I'm seeing across the cohort for what you're seeing from inside one. Worth a reply with a yes or a not-now?"

**Josh Braun:** Three tightens:
1. "Quick context" is filler. Cut.
2. "I'm one of the small group of operators who's been through FDA clearance for an AI medical device" ... overcomplicated. Try: "Some context: I got FDA clearance for an AI medical device a few years back."
3. "Worth a reply with a yes or a not-now?" is good. Binary, low-friction.

**Lavender:** Reading-grade level is too high. "Post-AI-clearance sales cycles aren't behaving like pre-AI ones" is a mouthful. Phone test: would a CEO parse this scrolling Slack in 2 seconds? Marginal. Simplify: "Selling an AI device is a different sale than selling a pre-AI one. Different payer conversation. Different evidence ask. Cycle stalls in a new place." 

Strongest sentence in the whole sequence: "I'd trade you what I'm seeing across the cohort for what you're seeing from inside one." Don't change it. That's the value transfer made concrete.

**Devin Reed (Gong):** Strong interest-based CTA. "Yes or not-now" beats every variant of "do you have 30 minutes Tuesday?" Credential drop is well-placed ... it frames the ask without leading the ask.

**Becc Holland:** "Compare commercial-trajectory notes" is jargon. CEOs don't think "commercial trajectory" ... they think "sales cycle," "go-to-market," "commercialization." Match their vocabulary. "Compare go-to-market notes" is sharper.

**Belal Batrawy:** "Quick context" = death. Cut. "I'm using a 90-day window to compare" ... bureaucrat-speak. Just say "For the next 90 days I'm comparing notes with..." Active voice.

**Jason Bay:** "Worth a reply with a yes or a not-now?" is excellent. Binary options reduce decision cost to near-zero. This touch should drive the bulk of your interest signals.

## Touch 3 ... Second DM (day 5-7, no reply)

> "One pattern I keep seeing in this cohort: founders are running post-AI-clearance sales like they're still selling the pre-AI device. The cycle stalls in a new place ... usually at the evidence ask, not the demo or the price. Probably 20 minutes of value either direction. Off base, or worth a yes?"

**Josh Braun:** "Probably 20 minutes of value either direction" ... you're sneaking the time ask back in. Cold-stage rule: no time number. Drop it or restructure: "Either direction, there's pattern recognition worth having." No minutes-count.

Otherwise solid. Touch 3 should bring NEW content, not bump. You added a specific failure mode (evidence ask, not demo or price). Good.

**Lavender:** Strongest touch in the sequence. Specific insight + soft CTA. "Off base, or worth a yes?" ... "off base" framing is brilliant (the negative-reverse trick). Keep it. But "worth a yes?" is vague ... yes to what? Specify: "Off base, or worth comparing notes?"

**Devin Reed (Gong):** The insight names a specific failure mode. That's the unlock. Touch 3 should make the prospect think "this person actually understands my business." *One escalation: reference a count if true. "Three companies I've talked to this month all stalled at the IFU/labeling conversation." If accurate, that's irresistible. Don't fabricate the number.*

**Becc Holland:** By touch 3 you've had 5-7 days to learn something about THIS prospect ... a LinkedIn post they made, news at their company, a hire. "Just saw X on your feed" opener for touch 3 would 2-3x the response. Hard at scale; flag for v0.2 with AI personalization.

**Belal Batrawy:** "Probably." No. Confident or cut. "Pattern recognition either direction." Done.

**Jason Bay:** Best touch in the sequence. The specific insight demonstrates Will operates in their world. If you only had ONE touch, it would be this one.

## Touch 4 ... Breakup

> "Last note from me. I'll close the thread. If sales cycle, payer conversations, or commercial motion ever becomes the question you want a peer read on, the door is open. Otherwise, good luck with the build."

**Josh Braun:** Clean breakup. Door open. Two refinements:
1. "Sales cycle, payer conversations, or commercial motion" ... too many things. Pick one. "If go-to-market for a post-FDA-AI-clearance device ever becomes the question..." more specific.
2. "Good luck with the build" is human. Keep it.

**Lavender:** Breakups have the highest single-touch reply rate in most sequences because they trigger reciprocity. Yours is fine. *Optional layer: add a network ask at the end ... "Curious, who else in your band would be worth me reaching out to?" Doubles as breakup.*

**Devin Reed (Gong):** Standard breakup. Will do its job.

**Becc Holland:** "Good luck with the build" is your strongest line here. Human, acknowledges them as a builder, not a target. Keep it.

**Belal Batrawy:** Tight. No fluff. Approved.

## Cross-sequence consensus

1. **The interest-based CTA structure is correct.** All five reviewers approve the kill-the-calendar-link decision.
2. **The strongest single line is the "trade you what I'm seeing across the cohort for what you're seeing from inside one" sentence in touch 2.** Do not change it.

4. **Manual-reply SLA after a positive signal matters more than the copy.** Jason Bay specifically: positive replies have a short half-life. Get the reply out within 4 hours of the "yes" signal landing.
