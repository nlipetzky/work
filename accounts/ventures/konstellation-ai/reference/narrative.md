# KAI Narrative and Voice Rules

The astronomical metaphor is the pitch frame, not decoration. Use it.

## The pitch (verbatim shape)

> Your organization is looking at the sky with old constellations. Here's what the new map shows.
>
> We introduce one new constellation at a time, at the pace your org can absorb. We usually start with the one you already half-recognize.
>
> Most clients stay in our orbit indefinitely ... that's not a downgrade, that's the right physics for most companies.
>
> If you want to escape orbit and own it outright, that's a deliberate maneuver. We'll tell you honestly whether it'll work.

## Three load-bearing consequences of the narrative

1. **Operated (DFY) is the default delivery mode.** Most organizations are still using the old telescope. They need an operator who can see the new constellations and point them out. Most clients will never fully transition their perception ... and that's fine. They're paying for the operator's perception, not just the build.
2. **Phased absorption is the only honest delivery model.** You can't show a client the whole new sky at once. Show one constellation that overlaps with what they already recognize, then widen the field of view.
3. **The pitch writes itself.** No agentic-architecture lecture required. The narrative does the work.

## KAI-specific voice rules

These apply on top of the universal craft principles in `practices/sales-and-gtm/CLAUDE.md`. Enforce both layers.

- **Astronomical metaphor is on-brand.** Orbits, constellations, clusters, sky-mapping, "old telescope vs new telescope," "escape velocity." Use it.
- **Do not lecture on agentic AI architecture.** The narrative carries the message; the architecture is implementation detail.
- **No dollar amounts in writing.** Per-unit incidental cost is acceptable ("Apify pulls ~$0.004 each"). Never aggregate to totals. Never quote retainers without Will.
- **The vendor list and volumes do the work.** Do not say "we're covering this" about costs. Saying it explicitly undercuts the message.
- **Buyer is a credentialed domain expert.** Biotech specialists, IP attorneys, founders, technical operators. Write to a peer who is good at their job and time-constrained.

## Companion universal rules

These come from `practices/sales-and-gtm/CLAUDE.md` and apply in all KAI-facing copy:

- No em dashes. Use ellipses.
- No emojis unless the recipient used one first.
- No corporate hedging.
- Peer-to-peer tone.
- Sign-off is first-name only. No corporate signature block.
- Specificity over abstraction.

## Two compounding ideas

The narrative ladders into KAI's core selling shape:

- **Cluster names solve the perception gap.** Buyers ask for "RevOps" not "Signal + Compass + Voice + Pulse + Canon." Will pitches a Cluster; the architecture underneath is Constellations.
- **Survey deliverable carries credibility.** Nick is rarely in the room. The Cluster name opens the door; the Survey output convinces the buyer. Will runs the human layer.
