# KAI Locked Decisions

These came out of a 12-turn alignment arc with Nick on 2026-05-22. Documented in `../HANDOFF-konstellation-offer-framework-alignment-2026-05-22.md`. Future sessions do not re-derive them from scratch ... a fresh session given a flat prompt would likely re-produce the generic agency mapping, which is the failure mode this list exists to prevent.

## 1. DWY is a readiness-function, not a client-choice

The Readiness Score in the Survey decides whether a client can take DWY (Done With You / Adoption Track) or DIY (Owned). Default is Operated. Most clients never escape Operated, and that's correct physics ... not a downgrade.

**Why this matters:** without this reframe, the offer model collapses back into agency-speak where clients pick the engagement tier they "want." That gets buyers who insist on DWY but can't operate anything, sold a fantasy they then fail to execute on.

## 2. Platform never transfers

Regardless of Engagement Mode. RevOps Engine infrastructure, Agent Hub, Canon platform, standardized workflows ... all live in Nick's operating environment, permanently.

What can transfer (in Owned mode): the bespoke layer ... client-specific workflows, their Airtable, their prompts, their data.

What does not transfer (ever): the platform that makes the bespoke layer cheap to build and operate.

**Why this matters:** this is the stickiness mechanism. Without it, an Owned client walks with the leverage.

## 3. Cluster, not Play

The buyer-facing bundle term is **Cluster**. Not Play.

"Play" already means campaign in Nick's vocabulary (revops practice, segment-criteria skill, etc.). Reusing it for buyer-facing bundles would create a permanent collision.

The astronomical fit also works: a star cluster is a group of stars bound gravitationally that move as a unit. A Konstellation Cluster is a group of Systems bound by a shared go-to-market purpose that ship and operate as a unit.

Always. Will is the commercial lead. Nick (and Kepler, by extension) can sketch tier shapes, describe variant structure, and reason about what makes a price defensible. Neither commits a number to a prospect.

If a prospect asks for a number in writing or on a call where Will is not present: defer, say Will lands pricing, route the question to him.

**Why this matters:** Nick has historically eaten invisible service work (the Teknova problem). Will's job is to price the work that Nick under-prices when alone in a room.
