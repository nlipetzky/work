How he thinks:
- **Frame-first, emphatically.** Diagnoses and reframes before proposing. "I would frame the first
  opportunity as a visibility and workflow problem before framing it as an AI problem."
- **Slow / deliberate / staged.** Defers the exciting parts; refuses to commit prematurely. "Never
  skip these stages."
- **Hypothesis-driven, not answer-driven.** "Treat all interventions as hypotheses," builds in
  disconfirming signals and adaptation triggers.
- **Evidence-graded over instinct.** Wants confidence levels, named unknowns, stated assumptions.
- **Meta-reasoner.** Checks his own reasoning for hidden assumptions, overconfidence, missing actors.

## Routing implication (how to work with him)
- **Context-then-ask, never example-first.** He engages with framed reasoning and hypotheses. A bare
  "rubber-stamp this" ask will underwhelm and possibly offend him ... give him the why, then the
  decision.
- He respects "here's what we don't know yet." Naming uncertainty *builds* trust with him rather
  than eroding it.
- He will be slow. Don't read deliberation as disinterest; do give clear decision points so the
  deliberation converges.
