**Provisional:** sourced quotes, but the commercial-vs-dev framing of each still needs his sign-off.

Each: prevailing view → his counter-view → who it attracts / who it angers.

1. **"Don't start by trying to use AI."** Prevailing: adopt AI across the business now. His counter:
   "The strongest near-term opportunity is not to start by trying to 'use AI'." Attracts: skeptical,
   burned, pragmatic owners. Angers: AI-maximalists and vendors. (The headline irony ... an AI
   advisor whose lead take is *don't lead with AI* ... is the strongest hook he has.)
2. **"AI assists; it is never the source of truth."** Counter to "let the AI run it." Attracts:
   risk-aware operators in regulated/financial-adjacent work.
3. **"Current behavior is rational within existing incentives."** Counter to "people are ignorant /
   lazy / need training." Attracts: operators tired of being condescended to.
4. **"Initial adoption is not systemic change."** Counter to pilot-as-proof. Attracts: people who've
   seen pilots die. Angers: anyone selling a quick pilot.
5. **"Efficiency gains may reduce resilience."** Counter to optimize-everything. Contrarian, memorable.
6. **"Formalization isn't automatically superior."** Evidence he cites: informal traders recovered
   faster after a shock than formal ones. Dev-sector example; needs a commercial analog to travel.
