## Commercially transferable (from the Absolute discovery)

1. **Tool-without-purpose.** Setup: org adopts a new system. Typical move: hand people the tool.
   Fails because "people are given another tool without a clear explanation of what it is for, how
   it helps them, what it replaces, and who is responsible for keeping it current." Instead: define
   purpose, replacement, and ownership before rollout.
2. **Fragmentation tax.** Setup: context scattered across phone/text/journal/Excel/QuickBooks/
   TeamUp/paper. Typical: add another system. Fails via manual re-entry, shared credentials, "status
   updates that depend on people remembering." Instead: build a single visibility layer before
   automating anything.
3. **AI-on-dirty-data.** Setup: owner wants analytics/AI on historical records. Typical: point AI at
   the data now. Fails ... "the AI analysis will either take longer, require more cleanup, or produce
   answers that still need heavy human interpretation." Instead: sequence visibility and clean
   records first, AI second.

## Underlying diagnostic instinct (from PROMEON, dev-sector framed)

These reveal how he thinks even though the domain is development economics:
- **Training fetishism** ... assuming behavior changes through knowledge transfer. Instead diagnose
  whether the constraint is skill, incentive, trust, risk, or governance.
- **Awareness assumption** ... confusing information exposure with changed incentives.
- **Technology solutionism** ... treating a tool as system-change. Ask "whose incentives and
  relationships does the technology alter?"
- **Pilot dependency** ... "mistakes a managed demonstration for systemic change."
- Plus: workshop substitution, activity-centric reasoning, linear causality.

## Update 2026-06-09 (from 2026-05-19 call) ... his core principle, in his own words

- **Facilitation-not-actor.** Go in as a catalyst, never the operator. "As soon as you leave the
  system collapses ... you're creating dependencies." Hard-won from earlier work where he went in as
  "the expert, I can fix this." This is the live, personally-owned version of PROMEON's facilitation
  doctrine ... use *this* framing with him and in copy, not the jargon.
