# **1\. Executive Summary**

This plan operationalizes SIM IP's deal origination engine beyond the founder network. The core thesis: SIM IP's $200M+ revenue guidance and 70%+ margins depend on a scalable pipeline of patent portfolio acquisitions and licensing engagements. Today, origination is concentrated in Erich Spangenberg's personal network. This plan builds a repeatable, measurable system that proves origination capacity scales with headcount — the single most important proof point for the IPO roadshow.

*Primary KPI: First independently-sourced portfolio acquisition generating licensing revenue within 12 months.*

The plan prioritizes five channels in order of expected ROI: (1) Patent Attorney Referral Network, (2) Founder-Led LinkedIn Outreach, (3) Content-Led Inbound, (4) Account-Based Marketing for top targets, and (5) Targeted Cold Outreach. Each channel has discrete ownership, metrics, weekly cadences, and kill criteria.

# **2\. Strategic Context**

## **2.1 Market Position**

SIM IP sits at the intersection of three converging tailwinds: the Unified Patent Court creating a cost-effective European enforcement forum, a pro-IP U.S. regulatory posture, and AI-driven patent analytics (via the Garden Intel merger) that collapse evaluation timelines from months to days. Fewer than 2% of issued patents generate any financial return. SIM IP's thesis is that this represents a massive mispricing of IP as an asset class.

## **2.2 The Origination Problem**

The S-1 identifies key-man risk explicitly: sourcing depends on management's experience, reputation, and network. Every channel in this plan is designed to retire that risk by creating origination pathways that are independent of, but complementary to, Erich's deal flow.

## **2.3 Competitive Differentiation**

* **Garden Intel AI integration:** Proprietary patent valuation at scale. No competitor can evaluate portfolios as fast or as cheaply.

* **Capital deployment speed:** SIM IP deploys its own capital. No committee approvals, no LP gatekeeping.

* **UPC-first enforcement:** 14-month injunction timeline vs. 3-5 years in U.S. district courts.

* **Cross-vertical flexibility:** Industry-agnostic. Semiconductor, healthcare, IoT, communications, AI — wherever the mispricing exists.

# **3\. Channel 1: Patent Attorney Referral Network**

**Priority: Highest. This is the primary origination engine.**

## **3.1 Strategic Rationale**

Patent prosecution attorneys sit on the largest pool of unmonetized IP in the world. They file and maintain patents for clients who often have no licensing strategy. These attorneys are incentivized to refer: every licensing dollar SIM IP generates strengthens the attorney-client relationship and validates the attorney's prosecution work. The pitch is alignment, not competition.

## **3.4 Execution Playbook**

**Week 1-4: Foundation**

* **Build referral one-pager:** Single page showing SIM IP's track record, Garden Intel's AI capabilities, typical deal structure, and referral economics. No jargon. Prosecution attorneys are not licensing attorneys.

* **Create CLE presentation deck:** 'Unlocking Dormant Patent Value: What Prosecution Counsel Should Know About IP Monetization.' Target 1-hour CLE format. Submit to AIPLA, IPO, and state bar associations.

* **Draft referral fee agreement template:** Standard terms for referring counsel. 5-10% of net licensing revenue on referred portfolios for 24 months. Legal review required.

**Week 5-12: Outreach**

* **Identify 3 partners per Tier 1 firm:** Focus on partners who manage prosecution portfolios for mid-market companies ($50M-$500M revenue). Use LinkedIn, Juristat, and PatSnap data to identify high-volume filers.

* **Personalized outreach sequence:** Email 1 (Day 0): Share a specific insight about one of their client's patents — e.g., 'Your client's US Patent X,XXX,XXX covers \[technology\] that we've identified \[N\] potential licensees for.' Email 2 (Day 7): Share the CLE deck with an offer to co-present. Email 3 (Day 14): Direct ask for a 20-minute call to discuss their clients' monetization opportunities.

* **Host quarterly IP Monetization Roundtable:** Invite 10-15 prosecution partners for a 90-minute virtual briefing on market trends, UPC developments, and anonymized case studies. Position SIM IP as the knowledge center.

**Week 13+: Conversion**

* **Portfolio evaluation pipeline:** For every referred portfolio, deliver a Garden Intel-powered preliminary valuation within 72 hours. Speed is the differentiator. Traditional evaluations take 4-8 weeks.

* **Structured feedback loop:** After each evaluation, debrief with referring counsel regardless of outcome. Build the relationship even when you pass on a portfolio.

# **6\. Channel 4: Account-Based Marketing**

**Priority: Medium. High effort per target, but highest conversion rate when executed well.**

## **6.1 Target Selection Criteria**

Select exactly 5 accounts per quarter. Each must meet at least 3 of these criteria:

* **Patent portfolio size:** 50+ issued U.S. patents.

* **Technology stage:** Early adoption phase with long replacement cycles (SIM IP's sweet spot).

* **No active licensing program:** The company files patents but has no in-house licensing team or external monetization partner.

* **Revenue profile:** $20M-$500M annual revenue. Large enough to have meaningful IP, small enough to lack dedicated IP commercialization resources.

* **Strategic alignment:** Portfolio overlaps with SIM IP's existing claim maps or Garden Intel's validated technology clusters.

## **6.2 Research Package (Per Account)**

Before any outreach, build a 2-3 page account brief:

* **Patent landscape:** Garden Intel analysis of the target's portfolio. Key patents, claim coverage maps, expiration timeline, citation network.

* **Monetization thesis:** Specific identification of potential licensees — companies operating in the target's claim space. Include estimated licensing revenue range.

* **Competitive intelligence:** Has anyone else approached this company? Are there existing licensing agreements? Any litigation history?

* **Decision-maker map:** CTO, VP of IP, General Counsel, CEO. Who controls IP strategy? Who has P\&L authority?

## **6.3 Multi-Touch Campaign (Per Account)**

**Touch 1 (Week 1): Insight Email**

Send the decision-maker a one-paragraph email with a specific, non-obvious insight about their portfolio. Example: 'Your patent family around \[technology\] has claim coverage that extends to \[adjacent application\] — an area where \[3 named companies\] are generating $\[X\]M in revenue without a license. We'd like to show you what we see.'

**Touch 2 (Week 2): LinkedIn Engagement**

Engage with the decision-maker's content. Share their company's press releases with commentary linking to IP strategy implications. Make them aware of you before the next direct touch.

**Touch 3 (Week 3): Warm Introduction**

Activate the attorney referral network. If a Tier 1 or Tier 2 prosecution firm represents this target, ask the referring partner for an introduction. If no existing connection, use the CLE/roundtable network.

**Touch 4 (Week 4): Portfolio Valuation Offer**

Direct offer: 'We'll run a complimentary Garden Intel portfolio screen. 72 hours, no commitment. Here's a sample of what the output looks like for a comparable portfolio.' Attach a redacted sample report.

**Touch 5 (Week 6): Executive Briefing**

If no response, send a one-page executive briefing customized to the target: estimated unrealized licensing revenue, comparable transactions, and a proposed next step. CC the referring attorney if one exists.

# **7\. Channel 5: Targeted Cold Outreach**

**Priority: Lowest. Use only for specific portfolio targets identified through Garden Intel data.**

## **7.1 When to Use**

Cold outreach is deployed only when Garden Intel's AI identifies a high-value portfolio with no existing relationship path. This is not a volume play. Each cold email is a research deliverable that demonstrates SIM IP has already done the work.

## **7.2 Email Template Structure**

One paragraph. Three sentences maximum per paragraph. Total length: 75-100 words.

**Structure:**

* **Sentence 1:** Name their specific patent or technology. ('Your US Patent \[number\] covers \[technology\].')

* **Sentence 2:** State the opportunity with a number. ('We've identified \[N\] companies operating in this claim space generating an estimated $\[X\]M in relevant revenue.')

* **Sentence 3:** Make the ask. ('We'd like to discuss a licensing program. Can we schedule 20 minutes this week?')

## **7.3 Disqualification Criteria**

Do not cold email if any of the following are true:

* **Active litigation:** The target is currently enforcing or defending patents. They have counsel and won't respond.

* **Existing NPE relationship:** Another monetization firm is already working the portfolio.

* **Portfolio near expiration:** Core patents expire within 3 years. Insufficient runway for licensing revenue.

* **Corporate policy against NPEs:** Some large tech companies have blanket policies. Don't waste the touch.

# **8\. Force Multiplier: Patent Owner Advisory Board**

*This is the single smartest addition to the plan. It creates a self-reinforcing referral network that scales without headcount.*

## **8.1 Concept**

Recruit 5-7 former CTOs, heads of IP, and university tech transfer directors who have successfully monetized through SIM IP or comparable platforms. They serve as referral sources, case study participants, credibility signals, and informal advisors. Each member who refers a portfolio that generates licensing revenue receives a referral fee, creating a network effect that maps directly to Garden Intel's data flywheel thesis.

## **8.2 Target Members**

* **Former CTOs:** Mid-cap tech companies ($100M-$1B revenue) who have been through IP monetization processes. They understand the value proposition from the owner's side.

* **University tech transfer directors:** Major research universities with large patent portfolios. They control thousands of patents and are perpetually under-resourced for licensing.

* **Corporate IP directors (retired):** Former heads of IP at Fortune 500 companies who maintain industry relationships.

* **SBIR/STTR alumni:** Federal grant recipients who generated patented technologies but lack commercialization infrastructure. This is a unique sourcing channel that no other monetization platform is working.

## **8.3 Structure**

* **Compensation:** Referral fee of 3-5% of net licensing revenue on referred portfolios. No retainer. Pure alignment.

* **Commitment:** One quarterly virtual meeting (90 minutes). Ad hoc referral introductions as opportunities arise.

* **Benefits to members:** Early access to Garden Intel portfolio analytics for their own IP. Networking with other advisory board members. Association with SIM IP's public market narrative.

## **8.4 Launch Timeline**

* **Month 1-2:** Identify and recruit first 3 members. Focus on relationships you already have from the SBIR/expert witness networks.

* **Month 3:** First quarterly meeting. Present SIM IP's thesis, demonstrate Garden Intel capabilities, establish referral process.

* **Month 6:** Expand to 5-7 members. First referred portfolio should be in evaluation by this point.

# **11\. Risk Analysis**

## **11.1 What Technical Risk Are We Retiring?**

Key-man origination concentration. The S-1's biggest structural risk is that deal flow depends on Erich Spangenberg's personal network. This plan builds parallel sourcing channels that produce attributable, independently-sourced pipeline. If it works, it transforms SIM IP from a founder-dependent model to a scalable platform — the distinction between a 5x and 15x revenue multiple at IPO.

## **11.2 What Does Failure Look Like?**

Six months in, fewer than 3 portfolios have been referred through any non-Erich channel. Content generates engagement but not qualified leads. ABM targets don't respond. The advisory board is assembled but produces no referrals.

## **11.3 Backup Plan**

If independent origination fails by Month 6, pivot to operating as Erich's technical diligence layer: evaluate portfolios he sources, build Garden Intel reports for his deal pipeline, and serve as the science/technical credibility in licensing negotiations. This limits upside but eliminates origination risk and still delivers value.

## **11.4 Milestone That Reduces Uncertainty**

First independently-sourced portfolio acquisition that generates a licensing revenue event. Not an evaluation. Not a meeting. A closed transaction with revenue. That's the proof point that origination scales beyond the founder.

## **11.5 What Increases Investor Confidence?**

A board presentation showing: (a) origination pipeline by channel with conversion rates, (b) average time from first touch to portfolio acquisition, (c) Garden Intel evaluation accuracy (predicted vs. actual licensing revenue), and (d) pipeline growth rate that demonstrates the model works with each incremental hire. This is the slide that moves SIM IP from 'Erich's company' to 'scalable IP platform' in the investor narrative.
