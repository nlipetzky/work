# The Play
## An outbound system for B2B companies in domains where targeting precision matters

**Nick Lipetzky · Konstellation AI**

---

## What it is

The Play is an outbound system I build and operate. One play runs at a time, producing verified target cohorts and conversion data against a roadmap you and I agree on at the start of each milestone.

What you get is a working production system, not hours, not strategy advice, not a list rental, not a managed-SDR team. It is the system underneath your outbound program. The unit of delivery is a play: one segment, one offer, one buyer, fully sourced, scored, sequenced, and measured.

It is built for B2B companies in complex domains where off-the-shelf data products do not produce defensible targeting. Life sciences, deeptech, regulated industries, specialized verticals. Companies where being precise about who you are reaching matters as much as how many.

## What the engagement produces

The engagement produces two streams of output: continuous, and milestone-driven.

**Continuous, once the play is active:**

- A running pipeline. The data engine (capture, classify, enrich, filter, contact) producing into your stack.
- Weekly written updates. Wednesday EOD. Progress against the active milestone, what is open on each side, what is coming.
- Conversion data. Reply rate, meeting conversion, opportunity creation, tied to the play that produced them.

**Milestone-driven, on the cadence the roadmap sets:**

Each milestone is a named deliverable, agreed in writing. Examples from real engagements:

- A first verified Tier A cohort, sourced and scored, ready for outreach.
- A Salesforce activity overlay, with saturation control wired into the send queue.
- Approved classification rules for a new segment or modality.
- A new discovery source brought online (patents, directories, scientific literature).
- A reporting layer that ties campaign performance back to play attribution.

Each milestone has its own roadmap of sub-deliverables and its own target completion based on the work involved. Most milestones run between three and eight weeks depending on scope; the roadmap names the target up front. The roadmap, not a calendar, sets the timeline.

When a milestone lands, the next one begins. One milestone runs at a time.

## Your role

The system produces the cohort, the data, and the infrastructure. You and your team use it to run outbound:

- Your team sends the outreach. The system produces the sequences and the cadence; your inboxes carry the send.
- A domain expert on your side approves classifications and verifies per-record fit. The cohort moves only after sign-off.
- You direct what milestones get prioritized through a one-page priority surface that lives in your Drive folder. The system is segment-agnostic; you supply the strategic direction.

## Where this fits, and where it does not

The Play works when:

- Outbound targeting precision matters more than raw volume.
- A domain expert on your team can approve classifications and verify segment fit on a roughly weekly cadence.
- Outbound is a strategic motion you intend to operate over quarters and years.

It does not work when:

- You are looking for a managed SDR team, a list-rental product, or outbound-as-a-service.
- You do not have a domain expert available for per-record approval.
- You expect mid-milestone strategic redirection. Direction is set at milestone start; once the roadmap is locked, it does not get renegotiated until the milestone closes.

## What is covered

Each month, the engagement covers:

- The active play: build, classify, enrich, filter, contact, and activation.
- Milestones running sequentially. One active at a time. A new milestone begins when the previous closes.
- Up to one platform integration or system build per milestone outside the active play roadmap (new sync, new reporting view, new automation).
- Weekly written updates and end-of-milestone review.
- Infrastructure that stays yours. Classification rules, integrations, data, and reporting persist beyond any individual milestone.

## What is not covered

Scope is named, not negotiated. Outside the engagement:

- **Tools and enrichment provider costs.** Enrichment APIs, data providers, contact verification, workflow infrastructure. Each milestone carries an estimated provider cost named in the roadmap. Provider costs are passed through at cost or billed directly to your accounts.
- **A second concurrent play.** Each additional active play is a separate engagement.
- **Net-new integrations beyond the one per milestone.** Additional builds inside the same milestone are scoped, sized, and added as line items.
- **Custom strategy work outside the play.** Brand positioning, content strategy, sales enablement, RFP responses, and similar are not part of the engagement.

Anything outside the engagement is scoped, named, and priced before it gets built. There are no quiet absorptions.

## How a milestone runs

- **Milestone start.** The milestone is named and locked in writing. I build a roadmap of the sub-deliverables and post it in your Drive folder, with a target completion based on the work involved. You confirm.
- **Production.** The priority surface in your Drive folder names what is active this week and what is queued for next. Each Wednesday EOD, a written update lands with progress against the roadmap and the target. Working sessions happen when there is concrete material to work through. Otherwise email and docs carry the cadence.
- **Milestone close.** When the deliverable lands, we review what shipped and what is next. The next milestone gets named and locked. The pattern repeats.

There is no artificial calendar deadline. A milestone closes when the work is done. If the target moves, it moves transparently in the weekly update with the reason, not silently at the end.

## Getting started

Getting going usually flows like this:

- We talk through what the first milestone should be and the segment it targets.
- I deliver the first roadmap a few days later with a target completion. You confirm or adjust.
- Production begins. The priority surface goes live in your Drive folder. Weekly updates start.

From there, the cadence runs.

The engagement is month-to-month. We can pause or wind down at the end of any active milestone in writing. No long-term commitment required.

## Operator notes (internal, not for prospect view)

Pass-through framing is the default in the document above because it surfaces costs cleanly and keeps the retainer focused on time and system value. In practice, the choice between bundled and pass-through is also a commercial lever. When a prospect or existing client pushes back on the retainer number, the move is to offer the bundled model at a higher fixed rate, or the pass-through model at a reduced rate. The variable cost goes onto whichever side has more flexibility in the negotiation.

Either model is workable. The framing chosen at engagement start depends on the conversation that gets us there. Existing engagements that evolved into a bundled shape without that framing being made explicit (Teknova is one) stay on the model they have until renegotiation, and the new framework is applied to the next conversation, not retroactively.
