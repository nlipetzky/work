# Offer: new-leader-abm-launch

**Client:** Pinpoint Software
**Play:** new-leader-abm-launch
**Date:** 2026-05-06

---

## Headline

ABM-in-a-box program for newly-hired demand gen leaders at mid-market B2B SaaS companies, helping them stand up a target-account motion within their first 90 days, timed to leverage the leader's onboarding mandate and budget reset.

---

## Audience

- **Persona:** Director, Head, or VP of Demand Generation at mid-market B2B SaaS companies (200-2,000 employees).
- **Context:** newly hired (≤6 months tenure), inheriting either a no-ABM or a fragmented-ABM motion, under board or CEO pressure to show pipeline impact within their first two quarters.
- **Pain we address:** the gap between "we need ABM" (board mandate) and "we have ABM" (operational system) typically takes 9-12 months to close without help. That window consumes the new leader's onboarding period before they can show wins, and the wins they do produce get attributed to inherited infrastructure rather than to them.

---

## The offer

- **Outcome:** an operational target-account motion running by day 90, with measurable pipeline contribution by day 120.
- **Mechanism:** 12-week embedded program combining target-account selection, ICP and signal definition, sequence build, technology configuration, and reporting setup. Senior ABM operator works directly with the leader and their team, not as a consultant deliverable producer.
- **Inclusions:** weekly working sessions; account-tiering workshop and tier list output; signal taxonomy and source mapping; sequence build for top tier; CRM and MAP configuration; pipeline measurement framework; handoff documentation the leader can present to their CEO.
- **Pricing or commercial frame:** fixed-fee, paid in two installments at kickoff and day 60. Engagement caps at 12 weeks with a one-month optional extension at the leader's request.

---

## Why now

- **Trigger:** demand gen leadership hiring in mid-market SaaS up 38% year-over-year per LinkedIn talent data; net-new program budget approval in mid-market SaaS skews heavily to Q1 and Q3 for newly-hired leaders.
- **Window:** the leader's first 6-9 months. After that, the onboarding budget evaporates and the leader is judged on the infrastructure they inherited.
- **Context:** post-2024 CAC compression has placed pipeline efficiency at the center of board-level scrutiny. New demand gen leaders are specifically being asked to show measurable improvements within their first two quarters, which compresses the time available to build operational capability.

---

## Proof

- **Named references:** Acme Robotics (300 employees; Sarah Chen hired as Head of Demand Gen Q3 2025; ABM motion live by day 78; $2.4M sourced pipeline by day 120). Lumify Analytics (450 employees; comparable timeline and result).
- **Measurable outcomes:** average time-to-first-ABM-meeting reduced from 14 weeks (industry baseline per Demand Gen Report) to 7 weeks for program participants. Average sourced pipeline in first 90 days post-launch: $1.8M weighted across last six engagements.
- **Third-party validation:** featured in Demand Gen Report's 2026 ABM Leaders Index.

---

## The ask

- **Primary CTA:** book a 25-minute call to walk through your first 90 days and identify whether the program fits your timeline.
- **Why this CTA:** new leaders are time-poor and resist long discovery calls. A 25-minute "first 90 days" framing matches the language of their internal onboarding mandate and demonstrates the program's operational discipline. The specific duration signals respect for their time and earns the click.

---

## Out of scope

- **Not pitched:** standalone ABM tool implementation (the program is platform-agnostic and runs on the leader's existing stack); ABM strategy consulting decoupled from operational delivery.
- **Not promised:** specific pipeline-dollar guarantees; closed-won outcomes. The program ends at sourced pipeline; downstream conversion is the leader's team's job and is not contracted.
- **Not for:** demand gen leaders with >18 months tenure (different problem, different sale); CMOs at companies where the demand gen function is unstaffed (requires a hiring conversation first); pre-Series-B companies without an established sales motion to feed.

---

## Confidence and gaps

- **Assumptions made:** "newly hired" is operationalized as ≤6 months tenure, based on typical onboarding-to-first-results horizons for marketing leaders. Leaders at 6-12 months tenure may convert; tracked separately.
- **Decisions against the brief:** brief described the offer as "an ABM consulting engagement"; reframed as an embedded operational program because consulting positioning loses to the leader's preference for hands-on delivery in their first 90 days. Brief did not specify a pricing model; chose fixed-fee over hourly to match the budget-approval format new leaders typically have available, and to avoid scope-creep risk on a 12-week engagement.
- **Open questions:** does the program require a minimum target-account list size to be effective? Sales has anecdotally said 50+ accounts; needs validation before we promise it in copy.
- **Signals not yet observable:** whether the prospect has already engaged a competing ABM consultancy. Currently undetectable without first-party signals; would meaningfully change positioning if known and is worth flagging to enrichment for the next iteration.
