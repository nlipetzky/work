# Schema: Role Brief

**Locked.** Output of pipeline stage 1. One per hire at `hires/<role-slug>/role-brief.md`.
Every line traces to something Nick said in discovery — no invented requirements.

Produce a markdown file with exactly these sections, in this order:

---

# Role Brief — <Working Role Title>

**Status:** draft | approved
**Engagement:** Upwork — hourly | fixed-milestone
**Date:** <YYYY-MM-DD>

## Why this role exists

The specific bottleneck. What's eating Nick's time today, and what changes once this
person is in seat. 2–4 sentences. Concrete, not aspirational.

## Outcomes owned

The 2–4 outcomes this person is accountable for. Outcomes, not activities ("the empty-leg
pipeline stays current," not "updates spreadsheets").

## In scope

Bulleted. The concrete work this person does.

## Out of scope

Bulleted. Work explicitly NOT theirs — the things Nick keeps, or that belong elsewhere.
This prevents scope creep and bad-fit applicants.

## Must-have skills

Bulleted. Non-negotiable. If an applicant lacks one of these, they're out.

## Nice-to-have

Bulleted. Strengthens an application but isn't a gate.

## What a bad hire gets wrong

The failure mode Nick is worried about, stated plainly. This drives the screening
questions in the job description.

## Engagement shape

- Model: hourly | fixed-milestone
- Estimated load: <hours/week or total project hours>
- Rate range: <$X–$Y> (the range Nick stated)
- Duration: ongoing | <N weeks/months> | one-off

## Success criteria

What "working out" looks like, with a checkpoint. Either a first-milestone definition
(fixed) or a 30/60/90 sketch (ongoing). Must be observable, not "good attitude."

## Screening signals

3–5 things an application must demonstrate for Nick to say "this person gets it." These
map 1:1 to the job description's screening questions. Each signal is checkable from a
written answer or portfolio, not an interview.
