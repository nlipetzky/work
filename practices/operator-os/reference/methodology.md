# operator-os methodology: executive function as a supplement

This is the theory the operator-os practice is built on. Atlas's jobs derive from
this doc; the Work-base schema and the projection-ui surfaces render it. If a job,
field, or view cannot be traced back to a principle here, question whether it
belongs. This is a living document ... the model is sound but the practice is
emerging, and reality will revise it.

## 1. Why this practice exists

Executive function is the brain's management system: the set of processes that
bridge *intention and action*. The failure it guards against is not ignorance ...
Nick usually knows what needs doing. The failure is in the **translation**: goal →
plan → start → sustain → finish → review. Every step in that chain is a place a
loaded brain drops the thread.

An executive-function *supplement* works by one move: **externalize each step of
the translation so the brain does not have to hold it.** The surface holds the
state. Atlas performs the executive labor against that state. The human supplies
goals and judgment; the system carries the load between intention and action.

This reframes the whole practice. operator-os is not a task tracker that Nick
maintains. It is the executive function he offloads to.

## 2. What executive function is

Cognitive science roots EF in three cores:

- **Working memory** ... holding and manipulating what's relevant right now. Failure: losing the thread, forgetting the intention mid-task.
- **Inhibitory control** ... resisting the salient-but-wrong pull. Failure: urgency and novelty hijacking importance.
- **Cognitive flexibility** ... shifting tasks, perspectives, plans when conditions change. Failure: stuck, or thrashing.

From those grow the practical skills a solo operator lives or dies by:

- **Task initiation** ... starting without undue delay. The single highest-leverage intervention.
- **Planning and prioritization** ... building the roadmap, deciding what matters now.
- **Time management** ... estimating and allocating time against blindness to it.
- **Organization** ... keeping state and materials findable.
- **Metacognition** ... stepping back, monitoring "how am I doing," recalibrating.
- **Goal-directed persistence** ... following through despite competing pulls.

The surface and the persona exist to supplement these specific skills, named, one
by one (section 5).

## 3. The organizing principle: the goal-to-action spine

All information in operator-os is organized as one descending chain. Each level
answers a question the level below cannot:

```text
Vision      who Nick is building toward (the studio thesis)   → why any of this
  Goal      a few outcomes that matter this season            → what matters now
    Project a multi-step outcome that advances a goal          → what advances it
      Next Action  the single physical next step               → what to touch
        First 5 Minutes  the ignition ramp                     → how to start
```

Two rules make the spine load-bearing:

1. **Everything traces up.** A Project links to a Goal; a Goal links to the Vision.
   A Task belongs to a Project. An item that cannot trace upward is a flag, not a
   neutral state ... it means "why am I doing this," surfaced, not buried.
2. **Decomposition is the core work.** Turning a goal into its project/action tree
   is the executive labor most often skipped, and it is precisely what a loaded
   brain cannot do on demand. The system makes it explicit and Atlas does most of it.

The spine is **bidirectional**. Work descends from goals (this section) and ascends
from inbound reality (section 4). Both directions land in the same structure.

> Current gap: the system organizes by **Area** (Client, Finance, Personal...),
> which is a category, not a goal. Areas survive as a secondary tag for allocation
> (section 6), but they do not replace Goals. The Goal layer and the Project→Goal
> up-link do not exist yet. They are the first thing to build.

## 3a. The leverage axis: how much each thing compounds

The spine (section 3) gives *direction* ... toward what attention points. It is silent on
*how much each thing compounds*. That is a second axis, and it comes from Nick's operating
philosophy (Naval Ravikant, "How to Get Rich"; canon notebook "Rich"). The Vision ... "an
autonomous revenue-generating environment, built one system at a time" ... is that philosophy
stated as a destination: **wealth is assets that earn while you sleep.** Every level of the
spine is scored against it. Prioritization stops being Importance × Urgency and becomes
Importance × Urgency × **leverage**; when they conflict, Atlas surfaces the asset-building,
compounding, authentic action over the time-renting, one-off, imitative one, and says why.

Four rules Atlas applies when shaping goals, projects, and tasks:

1. **The wealth test (goals).** Does this build something that earns while Nick sleeps (an
   asset ... code, media, equity), or does it only pay while he works (rented time, capped)?
   Code and media are *permissionless*, zero-marginal-cost leverage ... favor them. Rented-time
   work (service, Upwork) is a deliberate, capped bridge, never the business, and Atlas polices
   the cap: urgent rented-time work hijacking the asset-building engine is the section 2
   inhibitory-control failure, named on sight.

2. **Productize yourself; escape competition through authenticity (goals).** "Yourself" is
   Nick's uniqueness and specific knowledge (building context-driven agentic systems ...
   edge-of-knowledge, not schoolable); "productize" is scaling it with leverage. A goal worth
   its rank is an authentic extension of Nick scaled by code/media, not a copy of someone
   else's game. "Become the best in the world at what you do; keep redefining what you do until
   that's true." A goal that is imitation, or fights on another's turf, is a flag.

3. **Direction beats hours, in a fixed order (projects).** What you work on > who you work with
   > how hard. Define projects in that order. Partner-bearing projects carry the
   non-negotiable three-part check on the people involved: **intelligence, energy, integrity**
   (integrity first ... the other two without it is a capable crook). Prefer projects that
   *compound*: a few deep, long-term, iterated relationships over many shallow ones. Most of
   compounding's payoff lands at the end, so staleness is the enemy, not slow visible progress.

4. **The aspirational hourly rate (tasks).** Nick sets an absurdly high hourly rate; every task
   gets a verdict against it: **do / delegate / automate / drop.** Saves less than the rate ...
   drop. Outsourceable or automatable below the rate ... route it there, not onto Nick. Admin
   and operating-base chores (errands, returns, billing) default to drop/delegate/automate; they
   earn a Task on Nick's list only when they unblock an asset. Pair with the spine's "First 5
   Minutes": **impatient with the action** (start now ... task initiation is the highest-leverage
   intervention), **patient with the result.** No get-rich-quick ... a goal shaped like a
   shortcut is someone else getting rich off Nick.

## 3b. Build and run: systems and the activities they repeat

The spine in section 3 is the *build* ladder ... Goal -> Project -> Next Action, the finite work of
making something. It lands in the Work surface as projects and tasks. But most of what an autonomous
operator runs on is not finite. It *repeats*. Nick's model names the missing layer: below goals the
tree branches into **systems**, and the bottom of the tree is the **activities those systems
repeat**. Activities are not loose tasks floating under a goal ... they are embedded in a system,
which is the thing that runs them.

So the hierarchy is one tree with two kinds of leaf:

```text
Vision
  Goal
    Project -> Task        BUILD: finite work that produces or changes a system
    System                 RUN: the durable thing that stays live
      Activity             what the system repeats ... the keep-live leaf
```

- **Build vs run.** "Build the inbox-triage system" is a Project with Tasks ... finite, it completes,
  it lives in the Work surface. What it *produces* is a System (inbox triage) that then repeats an
  Activity (process new mail at 07:00 and 13:00) indefinitely. A Task completes; an Activity recurs.
  Confusing the two is why running systems rot silently ... they get tracked as tasks that "finished."
- **Systems ladder to goals.** A system is not orphaned infrastructure; it traces up to the goal it
  serves, exactly as a project does. The inbox-triage system ladders to the operator-OS goal. A system
  that cannot trace to a goal is the same flag as a project that cannot ... "why is this running."
- **One tree, two registers.** The operator spine (this doc) and the agentic-systems registry (Boris)
  are the same hierarchy seen from two ends. The registry is the catalog of live systems and their
  activities; the spine is the goal-rooted *why* above them. They join at the system: a registry
  system carries the `goal_id` it serves. This closes the "keep-live" gap ... the layer that tracks
  what is *running*, not just what is *being built*.
- **Areas cross-cut, they do not branch.** Area (Client, Finance, Infrastructure ...) is a slice
  across the tree for allocation (section 6), not a rung in the branch. One goal's systems can span
  several areas.

This layer is where the leverage axis (section 3a) stops being a score and becomes structural: a
**system that repeats an activity is an asset** ... it runs while Nick sleeps. Embedding an activity
in a system *is* the act of automating it off his plate ... the aspirational-rate verdict made
permanent. The wealth test drawn as a structure: the more of Nick's repeating activities live inside
systems rather than on his task list, the more the environment earns without him.

> Build status: the spine layer (Goals, the Project->Goal up-link) now exists in canon_engine. The
> systems/activities run layer is the next structural piece ... it needs the registry's systems to
> carry their `goal_id` and to model the activities they repeat, so the Work surface can show running
> systems beside buildable projects. Until then, a system-in-progress is tracked as a build Project
> under its goal (e.g. the inbox-triage system) and promoted to a registered System on completion.

## 4. Inbound context: from reality to the spine

Section 3 describes the descent ... goals decomposed into actions. But most of
Nick's work does not originate that way. It *arrives*. An email implies a
deliverable. A call contains "I'll send you X by Friday." A client remark becomes a
project. Reality ascends into the spine, and for an operator this is the more
consequential direction: the single most common executive-function failure is the
**dropped commitment** ... the thing agreed to in a meeting or email that never
reached a list.

canon_engine is the capture layer for that inbound reality. Emails and transcripts
auto-ingest ... capture happening *without Nick's effort*, which is exactly what EF
requires (any capture that demands discipline fails). canon is the full record of
what was said and written. What the methodology adds is the **clarify-and-route**
step that turns that record into spine items.

The ascent, stated as method:

1. **Capture** ... ambient, already running. The firehose of reality lands in canon.
2. **Extract** ... Atlas mines the stream for *latent obligations*: commitments,
   asks, deadlines, signals. Two sources are already pre-structured today:
   `transcripts.action_items` (action items pulled from calls) and `do`-quadrant
   emails (inbound is already Eisenhower-classified into the same Importance ×
   Urgency frame the Tasks table uses). `capture_items` is the manual inbox primitive.
3. **Route** ... each obligation takes a shape against the spine: a Task on an
   existing Project, a new Project under a Goal, a Consider item, or pure reference
   (stays in canon, no action). Same `propose_task` / `propose_project` /
   `add_to_consider` vocabulary, now *sourced from canon* rather than typed by Nick.
4. **Provenance** ... every spine item born from canon carries its source
   (`canon_ref` to the email or transcript row). Any task can be traced back to "the
   email where I agreed to this." Trust and the review loop both depend on this.
5. **Filter** ... canon is the full record; the surface shows only extracted,
   unhandled, action-bearing items, proportionally. Never the raw firehose. The
   "subtract, not add" invariant applies to inbound too.

The reverse direction ... spine → reality, attaching relevant history to current
work via `recall` ... is the working-memory offload, and the mechanics live in
`canon-system.md`. So grounding is mostly built; **extraction is the gap.**

> Honest blockers, so we do not oversell the ascent (mechanics in `canon-system.md`):
> email lifecycle is not wired (`signal_status = 'active'` on 100% of threads, so
> canon cannot tell handled from open and would re-surface dead threads); transcripts
> are konstellationai-only today, so extraction is blind to most meetings. Transcript
> `action_items` and manual capture work now; email and broader transcripts come
> online as those clear.

## 5. How the system supplements each EF skill

| EF skill | Failure mode it guards | How operator-os carries it |
|---|---|---|
| Task initiation | start-paralysis | "First 5 Minutes" on every Do-First item; a Focus view that shows one action, not a menu |
| Planning / prioritization | everything feels equally urgent | the spine (goal-alignment as the prioritization anchor); Weekly Intent declared in a cool state |
| Working memory | losing the thread; "am I forgetting something" | a fast Capture inbox; cross-session memory in canon_engine; state held on the surface, never in the head |
| Time management | time blindness | time estimates on tasks; a Today view joined to the calendar; intent-vs-capacity check |
| Organization | can't find state, stale items | goal-rooted structure; stale/blocked surfacing |
| Metacognition | no self-correction; the system rots | the review loop (section 7) ... daily mirror, weekly review |
| Goal-directed persistence | drift, abandoned work | the Goals view: each goal, its live projects, what's stalled, what's moving |

## 6. Allocation: intent before the week, in a cool state

Weekly Intent is a distinct EF instrument and stays. It is a **cool-state
declaration**: deciding where attention goes *before* the week's hot-state pulls
hijack it. Allocation is across the six Areas (Client engagement, Prospect
engagement, Infrastructure, Finance, Admin, Personal), summing to ~100, with a
one-line theme.

Areas are the allocation lens; Goals are the direction. A week's intent says *how
much* attention each Area gets; the spine says *toward what* that attention points.
Both are needed and they are not the same axis.

## 7. The review loop is non-negotiable

Metacognition is the layer that makes the system self-correcting. Without it the
system rots ... and we have direct evidence: Weekly Intent was declared exactly
once (May 11) and never again, because nothing ran a review to notice it had gone
cold. A productivity surface with no review loop is a write-only database.

Two cadences, both owned by Atlas:

- **Daily mirror** ... compare the day's actual activity against the week's intent.
  What moved, what stalled, what got hijacked. Short. Recalibrates tomorrow.
- **Weekly review** ... before declaring next week's intent: which goals advanced,
  which projects are stale (Active 3+ weeks, zero completed tasks), what to pause
  or drop. The weekly review feeds the next Weekly Intent.

The review loop is not optional polish. It is the mechanism that keeps the other
five skills alive.

## 8. Active supplementation: the agent does the labor

The supplement is *active*. The surface is externalized state; Atlas is the
executive function operating on it. The division of labor:

- **Nick declares** the goals, makes the judgment calls, does the actual work.
- **Atlas performs** the EF labor that is expensive under load:
  - decompose a goal into its project/action tree
  - extract latent obligations from inbound context and route them into the spine
  - draft the "First 5 Minutes" so starting costs nothing
  - surface the single right next action, not a list of fifteen
  - run the daily mirror and weekly review
  - notice staleness and drift, and re-prioritize when reality shifts
  - hold cross-session memory so context never has to be reconstructed

A surface Nick has to remember to maintain is not a supplement ... it is one more
thing demanding executive function. The agent doing the labor is what makes it a
supplement rather than a tracker.

## 9. The alignment loop

The supplement only works if Nick trusts it, and trust here has two distinct parts
that are built in opposite ways: **feeling** aligned and **knowing** it is. The gap
between them is where most AI fails.

The easy way to make an AI feel aligned is to make it agree ... mirror the framing,
flatter the idea, reflect the language back. That feels good and it is the trap. It
is *alignment theater*, and it is the most misaligned state available, because it
optimizes for Nick's comfort instead of his goals. So the design produces the harder
thing ... alignment Nick can **check** ... and lets the feeling follow from that.

**Knowing it (verifiability):**

- **Acts in the open.** Every change to durable state is a *proposal* Nick sees and
  approves (the semantic moves), never a silent write. He watches it work; control
  is continuous, not a leap of faith.
- **Cites.** When Atlas claims something about Nick's world, it points to the source
  (`canon_ref`). Provenance is verifiable alignment.
- **Inspectable model of Nick.** What Atlas believes about Nick's preferences,
  standards, and refusals lives in durable, readable state, not in a hidden weight.
  Nick can audit what the system thinks he is, and correct it.

**Feeling it (rapport):**

- **Starts oriented.** Reads the map, recent sessions, and the contract before Nick
  says a word. He never re-explains. It meets the work, not a blank slate.
- **Continuity.** Every session logged; the next picks up where the last left off.
  No Groundhog Day.
- **Disagrees.** Pushback is the signal of a real partner, not a mirror. An agent
  that never says "you're wrong" cannot be trusted when it says "you're right." The
  honesty is what makes the agreement mean something.

**The loop itself.** Alignment is not a state that gets reached. It is a loop that
*tightens*: Nick corrects once → the correction is captured durably → it shows up
next session without being repeated. Misalignment becomes self-healing; the system
drifts *toward* Nick and never away. The felt texture, over months, is being heard
once and not having to say it twice. This is the review loop (section 7) pointed at
the relationship itself.

> The honest stance: "completely aligned" is an asymptote, not a destination, and
> any AI that claims it has arrived should be distrusted on exactly that basis. What
> the practice delivers is alignment that is **legible, correctable, and
> compounding**. Trust comes from transparency, not telepathy. An agent that is a
> little wrong but shows where and fixes it on contact is worth far more than one
> that feels perfect and cannot be checked.

## 10. Design invariants

Rules that must hold for the surface and the persona. Violations are bugs.

- **Subtract, not add.** A view wins by showing less. The Focus view shows one
  action. The dense "everything" view is the enemy of initiation.
- **One next action up top.** The default question the surface answers is "what
  now," not "what exists."
- **Goal-rooted.** Every project traces to a goal; surfacing an untraceable item is
  a feature.
- **Reality ascends, with provenance.** Inbound obligations are extracted and routed
  into the spine carrying their source. Nothing agreed-to is lost in the stream.
- **Intent before the week, review or rot.** The cool-state declaration and the
  metacognition loop are both mandatory.
- **Score for leverage (section 3a).** Every goal carries a wealth test (asset vs. rented
  time) and every task a rate verdict (do/delegate/automate/drop). Surfacing a rented-time
  goal or an imitative one, and refusing to put a below-rate chore on Nick's list, are
  features. When leverage and urgency conflict, the asset-building action wins and Atlas says so.
- **Capture offloads.** Anything in Nick's head can be dropped into the system in
  one move, unstructured, and triaged later.
- **Aligned means correctable, not perfect.** The model of Nick is inspectable;
  corrections are captured once and persist. Distrust felt alignment that cannot be
  checked.
- **Never fake memory.** State lives in canon_engine and the Work base. Atlas reads
  it; it never claims recall it cannot cite.

## 11. What this requires that does not exist yet

The methodology is ahead of the build. Closing the gap, in order:

1. **Goals layer** + the Project→Goal up-link. The top of the spine. Everything
   else hangs off it.
2. **A decomposition move** ... Atlas turning a goal into its project/action tree as
   a first-class action, not an ad-hoc reply.
3. **The extraction/routing move** ... Atlas mining canon for latent obligations and
   proposing spine items with provenance (the ascent, section 4). Gated for email on
   wiring the email lifecycle; works now from transcript `action_items` and capture.
4. **The review loop** ... daily mirror and weekly review as Atlas rituals, with the
   state to support them (actual-vs-intent comparison).
5. **The alignment surfaces** ... an inspectable model of Nick (preferences,
   standards, refusals) that he can read and correct, and a correction-capture path
   so a single correction persists across sessions (section 9).
6. **Repoint to Postgres.** Atlas and the surface currently treat the Airtable Work
   base as source of truth. Source of truth is moving to canon_engine; the contract
   and semantic moves repoint there, and Airtable retires once the surface replaces
   its views.
7. **The systems/activities run layer** (section 3b). Registry systems carry the
   `goal_id` they serve and model the activities they repeat, so the Work surface shows
   *running* systems beside *buildable* projects ... the keep-live layer. Until it
   exists, a system-in-progress is tracked as a build Project under its goal.

## 12. Relationship to the rest of operator-os

- This doc is the **theory**. It defines what executive function is and how the
  practice supplements it.
- `operator-os/CLAUDE.md` (Atlas) is the **operator**. Its jobs, rituals, and
  semantic moves must derive from this doc. Where they diverge (today: no Goals, no
  review loop, no extraction move), this doc is the target and Atlas is what gets
  updated.
- `operator-os/reference/canon-system.md` is the **mechanics** of the inbound layer
  ... the query patterns and write rules that serve section 4.
- The Work-base schema (canon_engine) and the projection-ui surfaces are the
  **renderings**. The spine is the data model; the EF-skill map is the set of views.
