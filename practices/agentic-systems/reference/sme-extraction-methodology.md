## Why this exists (the leverage thesis)

A domain expert is the only thing that turns commodity outbound into defensible outbound. A generic agency says "we help companies grow." An SME-grounded engine says "I got the first US AI medical device approved; I know exactly why your robotics company can't grow." Same channel, same message structure, different gravity.

The job of SME extraction is not biography. It is identifying:

1. **What the SME uniquely knows** (proprietary pattern recognition)
2. **Who the SME uniquely has credibility with** (peer audience definition)
3. **What the SME uniquely can promise** (defensible deliverable)
4. **What stories the SME uniquely can tell** (war-story credibility)
5. **What network the SME uniquely has** (warm path + name-drop fuel)
6. **What contrarian POVs the SME uniquely holds** (cold-hook attention capture)
7. **What the SME refuses to do** (positioning by negation)

Each one of these maps to a specific outreach lever. None of them are extractable from public sources; all require structured conversation with the expert.

The thesis: the engine output is only as good as the SME profile feeding it. Time invested up front in extraction compounds across every play, every cold message, every Diagnostic delivered.

## The two extraction layers

### Layer 1: Structured initial intake (one-time, ~90 min)

A recorded long-form conversation walking the artifact list below. Operator (or Hermes when wired) drafts v0 of each artifact from the transcript. SME reviews and approves through the expert-liaison loop.

Cadence: once per SME, at engagement start. Re-run when the SME's positioning materially shifts (new credential, new domain pivot, new venture).

### Layer 2: Continuous extraction (every interaction)

Every real call the SME runs (with a prospect, an advisor, an internal partner) is a chance to capture new pattern data, new war stories, new vocabulary. The Learnings loop is the mechanism:

- Transcript or notes → operator extracts Learnings → Hermes routes approval → approved Learnings update the relevant SME profile artifact.

Without Layer 2, the SME profile freezes at intake and the engine compounds error. With it, the SME profile sharpens with every conversation.

## The complete SME artifact list (proposed taxonomy)

Eleven artifacts. Each is a temporal container per the artifact-discipline canon. v0 drafted from intake transcript; v1+ refined through the Learnings loop.

### 1. SME Identity Profile

The bio that fuels trust. Career arc, credentials, titles held, named accomplishments. The static-ish foundation.

**Feeds:** about-page copy, LinkedIn-profile guidance, signature blocks, "who is on the call" briefings.

### 2. SME Credibility Map

Granular peer-credibility by audience segment. Will has different credibility weight with surgical robotics founders vs. rehab robotics founders vs. IP attorneys vs. SaaS operators. Each segment row: which segment, what specifically gives the SME credibility there, strength rating, what kind of conversation lands.

**Feeds:** ICP definition, segment prioritization, channel selection per audience.

### 3. SME Pattern Library

The things the SME has seen repeatedly that less-experienced people miss. "I've watched this fail N times for Y reason." Each pattern: setup, what people typically do, why it fails, what the SME does instead. These become the diagnostic insights that anchor the Survey/Diagnostic.

**Feeds:** Diagnostic deliverable content, audit framework, "we look at X" credibility moments in cold copy.

### 4. SME Hot Takes / Contrarian POV

Opinions the SME holds that cut against industry conventional wisdom. Each hot take: the prevailing view, the SME's counter-view, evidence the SME would cite, who it tends to anger and who it tends to attract. These are cold-hook gold.

**Feeds:** cold copy openers, content posts, podcast pitches, the "wait, what?" moment that earns a reply.

### 5. SME War Stories / Case Files

Specific named-or-anonymized stories the SME can tell. Each story: setup, conflict, what the SME did, outcome, lesson. The "I helped solve this exact thing" credibility anchor.

**Feeds:** proof in cold copy (when permitted), discovery call narrative, Diagnostic case examples, follow-up content.

### 6. SME Network Map

Who the SME knows, in which categories, with what relationship strength (cold acquaintance / collaborator / close ally / advisor / board / friend). Includes named accounts where the SME has a personal-history exclusion.

**Feeds:** warm-intro path overlay on cold list, name-drop authorization in copy, named-account exclusion list.

### 7. SME Refusal List

What the SME won't do, who they won't work with, what segments they exclude. Positioning by negation. (For Will: skip enterprise, skip law firms.)

**Feeds:** ICP disqualifier rules, sales-call hard-no scripts, engagement-shape boundary.

### 8. SME Vocabulary / Voice

The words, phrases, framings the SME naturally uses. How they describe problems. How they describe themselves. Specific phrases that are themselves credibility anchors ("first US AI medical device approval"). Things they refuse to say.

**Feeds:** cold copy authenticity (the SME has to sound like themselves), LinkedIn profile language, voice rules for the venture.

### 9. SME Bias Map

What the SME believes that they may not yet have evidence for. Where their gut is leading. Useful so the operator can flag "Will's hypothesis, unvalidated" vs. "Will's lived experience, treat as truth." Honesty-with-self territory.

**Feeds:** Learning Questions doc (the bias becomes a hypothesis tested in market), copy-test prioritization.

### 10. SME Time Boundaries

How the SME wants to spend time. What they refuse to do (Will: avoid excessive client meetings, async from clear inputs). What they only do in batch. Energy budget per week.

**Feeds:** engagement-shape design, cadence definition, Trajectory phase pacing.

### 11. SME Decision-Making Profile

How the SME thinks. Slow vs. fast. Data vs. instinct. Frame-first vs. example-first. Risk posture. How they want options presented.

**Feeds:** how Hermes routes approval asks, how the operator presents trade-offs, how the SME wants the Slot Report shaped.

## How SME profile combines with venture catalog

The SME artifacts on their own are dormant. The venture catalog on its own is generic. The combination is the lead-gen engine.

Concrete combinations:

- **Credibility Map × Catalog Clusters → which Cluster lands hardest with which audience.** "Given Will's credibility, the RevOps Cluster lands hardest with founder-CEOs at $10-30M medical device robotics companies."
- **Refusal List × Catalog → which Clusters/Systems are on the menu at all.** Will won't sell to law firms; the Cluster offering excludes IP-services use cases.
- **Hot Takes × Cluster Outcome → cold copy hooks.** The hot take is the opener; the Cluster outcome is the offer. Bridge in the middle.
- **Pattern Library × Diagnostic → audit specificity.** Will doesn't audit in general; he audits for the failure patterns he knows cold.
- **Network Map × ICP → warm-path overlay.** Cold list gets enriched with warm-intro candidates.
- **Vocabulary × Voice Rules → cold copy authenticity.** The expert has to sound like themselves, not like a marketing department.
- **Time Boundaries × Trajectory → realistic engagement pacing.** Phase cadence respects what the SME will actually do each week.
- **Decision-Making Profile × Approval Loop → how Hermes routes.** A frame-first SME gets context-then-ask. An example-first SME gets the example first.

## Where these artifacts live (decision logged 2026-05-27)

Two-layer split:

**Registry and lineage layer:** liaison base `appbFsdqrC5vnxuIR`, `Expert Artifacts` table. Each of the eleven SME artifacts gets one row. The row carries Name, Version, Status, Owner, Approver, Approval Date, Channel, Signature Ref, File Path. Exchanges and System Artifacts tables in the same base track approval routing and downstream engine binding.

**Content layer:** markdown files in the venture's artifacts folder, e.g. `accounts/ventures/konstellation-ai/artifacts/sme-<artifact>-<expert-slug>-vN-<date>.md`. The Expert Artifacts row's File Path field points to the markdown. Narrative artifacts (Identity, Voice, Time Boundaries, Decision-Making Profile, Hypotheses) live as full markdown. Tabular artifacts (Credibility Map, Pattern Library, Hot Takes, War Stories, Network Map, Refusal List) live as markdown structured lists for v0; individual artifacts get promoted to structured storage when scale demands.

**Promotion path:** when the liaison base ingests into Canon (`canon_artifacts` in the `canon_engine` Supabase project), SME profile artifacts ride along automatically. The liaison base remains the operator-facing surface; Canon becomes the analytical layer that enables cross-engagement and cross-SME pattern queries.

The earlier draft of this methodology proposed minting a new "Expert Profiles" base. That was wrong because it overlooked the existing liaison base. The corrected decision uses what's already built. See `practices/sales-and-gtm/reference/sme-storage-architecture-v0-2026-05-27.md` for the full storage architecture artifact.

## How extraction runs (proposed sequence)

1. **Operator prepares.** Reads existing venture context (CLAUDE.md, catalog, narrative, locked decisions). Drafts an intake interview script tuned to the SME and venture.
2. **Intake conversation.** ~90 minutes, recorded and transcribed. Walks the eleven categories. Open-ended; operator probes; SME talks.
3. **Operator drafts v0 artifacts.** All eleven, from transcript. Lean drafts. Mark unknowns explicitly.
4. **Hermes routes approval.** Eleven artifacts is a lot to approve at once; batch into 2-3 tranches by approval shape (identity-credibility, pattern-stories-hottakes, vocabulary-bias-time-decision). SME approves or edits.
5. **Artifacts bind to engine.** Approved artifacts feed offer extract, ICP titles, segment criteria, cold copy, Diagnostic content, Trajectory pacing.
6. **Continuous Layer 2 runs forever.** Every prospect call, every internal session produces Learnings that update the relevant artifact.

## Open design questions

1. **Intake interview script artifact.** Should there be a reusable structured-interview script at the practice level (one for the operator to apply per SME)? If yes, this is itself a new artifact type. Recommend yes; first draft after this methodology is approved.
2. **Hermes interface to SME for intake.** Does the intake interview itself run through Hermes (Hermes asks questions, SME answers async), or does the operator (Nick) conduct it live and feed transcripts to Hermes for drafting? Recommend operator-conducted for v0; live conversation is higher bandwidth than async.
3. **Multi-SME ventures.** KAI has Will (sole SME). Future ventures may have multiple. Does each SME get a full artifact set, or do shared artifacts (Voice, Vocabulary) become venture-level with per-SME overlays?
4. **Public-facing fragments.** Some SME artifacts (Hot Takes, War Stories with permission) are also content-publishing fuel. Should an "approved for publication" flag live on each fragment? Recommend yes; treat it as a sub-status on the artifact.
5. **Bias Map sensitivity.** The Bias Map names where the SME might be wrong. SMEs may resist this artifact. Recommend framing as "hypotheses to test in market," not "biases" in user-facing language.
6. **Refusal List exposure.** Some refusals are personal-history (named-account exclusions). These should not surface in any client-facing artifact, only in internal sourcing rules. Recommend a sensitivity flag per row.
