# Forge — Constellation

Definitive artifact for the Forge constellation. If anything else disagrees with this file about what
Forge is, this file wins. Constellation #4 of the eight.

## What Forge is

Forge is how the business makes new things. It is the production engine: it turns a need into a new,
working, durable Asset, whether that Asset is an agentic system, a skill, a workflow, a piece of
content, or a context-engineering spec. "Capabilities and content" are one constellation because they
are one act — production — and one output type: the catalog's Asset (a workflow, a database, a context
spec, content, or a surface). Forge builds Assets and composes them into Systems.

For an owned-asset studio, Forge is close to the entire product. Everything the studio sells or owns is
a Forge output, and the speed and quality with which Forge turns a need into a working system is the
studio's core competitive metric — the "build" in "owned-asset studio." A business that can't build
new capability is frozen: it can only sell what it already has and can't evolve as the market or the
offer moves.

The capability is not "make something." Making is easy; making the *right* thing, well, repeatably, is
hard. Forge is two hard things: turning a fuzzy need into a correct built thing (the spec-to-build gap
— getting it right, not just shipping), and doing it repeatably with quality so building is not a
heroic one-off each time (reusable blocks and method, not reinvention). For an agentic studio
specifically, the built thing must itself be durable and operable (context-engineered, registered),
not a throwaway script.

This constellation is self-referential: the agentic-systems practice (Boris) is Forge's primary
operator, and the methodology, registry, and skills built in this very work are Forge's own assets.

Boundary: Forge builds. It does not decide what to build (Compass), find the need outside (Signal),
communicate the built thing (Voice), sell it (Pulse), or operate and grow it once running (Garden and
the owning constellations). Forge builds Canon Ingestion; once it runs, Canon operates it.

## What good looks like

We know Forge is delivering value when:
- A new capability or piece of content goes from need to shipped fast, without a heroic from-scratch
  build each time.
- What gets built is the right thing: it solves the actual need, works, and is durable (registered,
  operable), not a throwaway that rots.
- Building reuses existing blocks (skills, templates, methodology, prior assets) instead of starting
  cold — production compounds.
- The studio can take on a new system or offer and produce it predictably; build capacity is a known
  quantity, not a gamble.
- Quality is consistent: built things meet the studio's bar (schema-compliant, on-method) without
  bespoke heroics.

You feel Forge's absence when every build is a from-scratch heroic effort, when built things are
throwaway and unregistered and rot, when the studio can't take on new work because building is
unpredictable, when quality varies wildly, or when capabilities sit half-built for lack of repeatable
production.

## Systems (operational — live status in the base)

Three systems sharing the production substrate: the methodology, the skill/template library, the
registry, the artifact corpus, and the build environment.

| System | Class | Coverage | Produces which "good" |
|---|---|---|---|
| Forge Design | Core | Partial | the right thing; solves the actual need |
| Forge Production | Core | Partial | shipped fast and durable |
| Forge Toolkit | Supporting | Partial | reuses blocks; consistent quality; compounds |

**Headline: Forge Production — the frontier is the leap from heroic to repeatable/autonomous
production at scale.** Forge is the studio's **strongest** constellation (it's a build studio), so its
gap is not a missing watch-system; Design and Toolkit are among the most-developed capabilities in the
whole org. Forge departs from the keep-live pattern: the frontier here is scale and autonomy of
production, not decay-watching.

## Dependencies

- **Forge depends on** Compass (what to build — Design wired to `compass-planning`) and Canon (the
  knowledge and prior assets to build from; wired to `canon-context-service`).
- **Consumed by everyone.** Every other constellation's systems are Forge outputs. Forge is upstream of
  all building; only Canon (the substrate it builds on) sits beneath it.

## Open questions

1. **Production autonomy.** The leap from human-in-the-loop heroic builds to repeatable / partially
   autonomous production is the main Forge investment. Define what "autonomous build" means and where
   the human stays in the loop.
2. **Content vs capability split.** Capabilities (systems/workflows) and content (copy/media) share the
   production act but differ in medium. If content production grows enough to warrant its own system,
   split it out; for now they share Forge Production.
