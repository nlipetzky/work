THE FRAMEWORK, IN ONE PARAGRAPH

We sell a Konstellation Catalog. Inside the catalog are eight Constellations, each one anchored to something every business has to do regardless of industry... Canon (knows what the business knows), Compass (decides what's next), Signal (finds opportunities), Forge (builds capabilities), Voice (speaks and listens), Pulse (moves transactions), Guard (protects compliance), Garden (grows what exists). Each Constellation contains Systems, the deployable SKUs, the thing a client actually buys.

Buyers don't think in Constellations though. They think in old functional terms. So we also offer Clusters... pre-bundled groups of Systems across multiple Constellations, named in buyer language. The RevOps Cluster is Signal + Compass + Voice + Pulse + Canon. The Customer Expansion Cluster is Garden + Canon + Voice. You sell Clusters. The architecture underneath is Constellations.

HOW A SALE WORKS

1. You sell a paid, time-boxed GTM Survey, about 2 weeks, productized. Output: system design, readiness score, data architecture audit, and a recommended Trajectory. The Survey is itself a small revenue event, not a free discovery cost we eat.

2. Survey output becomes the Engagement Plan (the Trajectory). It names which Clusters and Systems land in the first 90 days, with a backlog of what comes after.

3. The client signs into one of three Engagement Modes:

   - Operated (default). We build, we run, they see a Surface. Retainer covers everything. Most clients land here and stay here. This is the right physics, not a failure mode.
   - Adoption Track (gated add-on). Milestone-driven program to transfer ownership of the bespoke layer to a named operator on their side. Real possibility of not completing. Priced separately.
   - Owned (destination). Only reached by completing an Adoption Track. Even then, the platform never transfers... only the bespoke layer.

4. Rare premium SKU: Implementation Bootcamp for prospects who want to learn to do it themselves. Coaching rates, time-boxed, capped hours. Default posture is to refuse and redirect to Operated.

THE NARRATIVE FOR THE PITCH

You're looking at the sky with old constellations. Here's what the new map shows. We introduce one new constellation at a time, at the pace your org can absorb. We usually start with the one you already half-recognize. Most clients stay in our orbit indefinitely... that's not a downgrade, that's the right physics for most companies. If you want to escape orbit and own it outright, that's a deliberate maneuver and we'll tell you honestly whether it'll work.

That's the entire pitch in five sentences. You don't need to teach anyone the architecture to close.

WHAT THIS SOLVES

- The Teknova problem (sweetheart pricing, invisible service work I eat) doesn't repeat. The Survey makes the data architecture, change management, and strategy work explicit and priced.
- Buyers who think they want DWY but can't operate anything don't get sold a fantasy. The Readiness Score in the Survey filters honestly. Most reroute to Operated.
- You can sell without being technical. Cluster names and the orbit narrative do all the work. The Survey deliverable carries the credibility.
