# Partner-Led Discovery Protocol

Practice-level, entity-agnostic. Used by any venture (KAI today, future entities) to take a
partner-sourced fuzzy opportunity from first conversation to "ready to build the first
intervention" ... run mostly by the venture partner so Nick stays on systems.

This is the leverage mechanism: build it once, every partner runs it, Nick shows up only at
the gate. It is *client current-state discovery*, distinct from SME extraction (which captures
the expert as engine fuel). Do not conflate.

## The delegation boundary

The protocol exists to define one line: **what a partner must deliver before Nick touches an
opportunity.** Everything before the gate is the partner's. Nick enters at the gate to build.

- **Partner** (the credentialed expert who anchors an entity ... one per entity): takes the
  calls, runs the conversations, fills the Discovery Snapshot. Owns the relationship and the
  human-heavy work. The partner *is* what we sell ... we offer access to their expertise, and
  the agentic system delivers it invisibly behind them.
- **Nick**: builds and runs the agentic intervention once the gate is satisfied. Does not do
  discovery, does not chase the client, does not fill the snapshot.

Failure mode to guard against: the partner punting the hard part back to Nick before the gate
(seen verbatim in an early call as "Nick leads, partner learns"). An incomplete snapshot
bounces back to the partner, never forward to Nick.

## The funnel

1. **Conversation.** Partner takes calls. High volume, brute force, no separate qualification
   call (high-volume calls first, refine later). We sell the partner's experience and judgment,
   delivered through agentic systems ... not "AI" abstractly.
2. **Free diagnostic.** The entity's branded assessment (KAI calls it the Survey ... a ~9-step
   Theory-of-Constraints assessment). Free, used as a volume conversion tool ("30 free
   diagnostics → 5 clients"). Output feeds the Discovery Snapshot.
3. **Gate — Definition of Ready.** The snapshot's must-have fields are complete. Hand to Nick.
4. **Build the first intervention.** Only once a customer in the channel is interested
   (build-on-demand ... no pre-building before there is a buyer). Smallest scope that hits the
   one bottleneck.
5. **Run / manage.** Managed agentic capacity on a retainer. The client gets the outcome (a
   promised result / digest), not the agents. Outcome-based.

## Theory-of-Constraints frame

Every diagnostic converges on the same shape (Goldratt, *The Goal*): assess the operation →
find the **single highest-impact bottleneck** → scope a small intervention with a **concrete
promised result** → apply managed agentic capacity until the result lands → reassess → next
bottleneck. "Make a promise, keep a promise."

## Offer-shape guardrail (load-bearing)

The diagnostic must converge on **managed agentic capacity applied at a bottleneck**, NOT a
custom software build. Nick is explicitly not selling custom software development. If a
diagnostic drifts toward "build them a bespoke app/portal," the gate catches it and the
opportunity is re-scoped to a bottleneck-sized agentic intervention, or flagged as off-model.

## Zero-partner-overhead rule

Any manual process the partner must do will fail. Nick automates the partner's actions into a
daily "do these things" email; the partner takes calls and executes. The CRM / surface is
operated for the partner, not by them.

## Pricing

Routes through the engagement's commercial lead. The protocol never commits numbers. Tier
shapes and retainers are the commercial lead's to set.

## The gate

See `discovery-snapshot-template.md`. An opportunity is proposal/build-ready when the
snapshot's Definition-of-Ready checklist is fully answered. Missing any must-have → back to the
partner.
