# Sales fundamentals

Operating principles for sales-and-gtm work, captured as they surface. Practice-level. Apply to every engagement that consumes this practice. Extend this file when a new fundamental gets named; don't bake them into engagement-specific docs.

## Always book the next meeting

No meeting ends open-ended. Every meeting closes with one of two outcomes:

1. **The next meeting is on the calendar.** A specific date and time, agreed in the room, before anyone hangs up.
2. **Agreement to move forward.** The prospect commits to a defined next step (typically a proposal). That commitment triggers proposal creation, and the proposal is presented in the next meeting ... which is also booked before the current meeting ends.

If neither happens, the meeting did not close. That is a failure state, not a normal one.

## "Paused" is a problem state, not a parking spot

If a prospect says "let's talk in a couple weeks," that is not paused. That is a specific date in the future ... book it now.

"Paused" in the CRM means: we tried to book the next meeting, we couldn't, and we don't have a defined re-engagement trigger. It is rare and it gets attention.

When a prospect says they need time before the substantive meeting, book the substantive meeting at the future date in the same exchange. Don't ask for permission to follow up. Propose the time.

## Why this matters

Momentum dies in the gap between meetings. Every day a prospect spends not-on-our-calendar is a day they re-prioritize, get pulled into something else, or forget what we discussed. The CRM exists to make sure that gap is named, dated, and short.
